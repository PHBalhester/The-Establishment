## Why Three Tokens?

This is the question everyone asks. Here's the answer:

### The Dual External Tokens (CRIME + FRAUD)

You need **two** tokens to create tax asymmetry. The protocol assigns one as cheap and one as expensive each epoch. This asymmetry is what temporarily breaks the soft peg and creates the arb windows that generate revenue.

### The Reward Token (PROFIT)

Why PROFIT? Well... because that's how you make it in Crypto. You do some Crime, you get some Profit. Just ask the devs on PumpFun.

PROFIT serves a dual purpose:

1. **Reward vehicle:** Staking PROFIT rewards you with SOL. This gives participants a reason to hold and stake their PROFIT, which in turn provides stability and depth to the ecosystem.

2. **Conversion sink:** CRIME and FRAUD flow into the conversion vault to acquire PROFIT, removing tokens from circulating supply.

The three-token design creates a self-reinforcing loop where every participant needs the others.

## Transfer Restrictions

CRIME, FRAUD and PROFIT all use **Token-2022 with transfer hooks**. Every single transfer of these tokens, without fail, passes through the protocol's Transfer Hook Program for validation.

The rule is simple: **at least one side of every transfer must be a whitelisted address** (a pool vault, the Carnage Fund, the conversion vault or the staking vault).

What this means in practice:

- **No wallet-to-wallet transfers.** You cannot send CRIME, FRAUD, or PROFIT directly to another wallet. Yes this sucks. Yes you will have to get over it.
- **Everything routes through pools.** Every buy, sell or swap goes through the protocol's approved pools and vaults. Two things are certain in life, remember?
- **No OTC deals.** As above. Pay your damn taxes.
- **All activity generates fees.** Since every token movement goes through a pool, every movement contributes to trading volume and taxes.

The whitelist is managed by a whitelist authority that is retained at launch behind a time-locked multisig. This allows the team to add new whitelisted addresses if needed (e.g. for protocol upgrades). The whitelist authority will be burned once the protocol has proven stable and been externally audited, making the whitelist permanently immutable.

> *"Some call these restrictions draconian. I call them 'experimental controls.' A proper scientist does not let the subjects tamper with the apparatus."*
