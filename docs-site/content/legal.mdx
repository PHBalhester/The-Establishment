## III. Risk Disclosure (The Scary Part)

### You Can Lose Everything

Interacting with the Factory involves substantial risk of financial loss, up to and including the total loss of all assets you commit to the protocol. This is not a theoretical warning placed here by cautious lawyers — it is a genuine, material possibility that you should take seriously. Decentralised finance protocols are experimental technology. Smart contracts may contain bugs, exploits, or unforeseen interactions despite best efforts at testing and security review.

### Specific Risks Include (But Are Not Limited To):

**Smart Contract Risk.** The protocol's on-chain programs are software. Software has bugs. While the Factory's code has undergone extensive testing and automated security analysis, no formal third-party audit has been completed at the time of launch. A professional security audit is planned and will be funded from protocol treasury. Until then, you should treat the protocol as unaudited experimental software — because that is exactly what it is. Once program upgrade authorities are burned, bugs cannot be patched. This is by design, but it is also a risk.

**Market Risk.** Token prices are determined by supply and demand within automated market maker pools. Prices can and will fluctuate — sometimes rapidly, sometimes dramatically, and occasionally in directions best described as "catastrophically downward." There is no floor, no circuit breaker, and no Doctor standing by the emergency lever (despite what the marketing video suggests).

**Liquidity Risk.** The protocol operates with finite liquidity. Large trades will experience price impact. In periods of low activity, liquidity may be insufficient for you to exit your position at a price you consider acceptable, or at all.

**Tax Mechanism Risk.** The protocol imposes asymmetric trading taxes that change every epoch via verifiable random function. You may execute a trade during a favourable tax regime only to find conditions have shifted by the time your transaction confirms. The protocol's tax mechanism is a feature, not a bug — but it is a feature that costs you money.

**Bonding Curve Risk.** Initial token distribution occurs via bonding curves with a 48-hour deadline. If either curve fails to reach its target, both curves fail and refund mechanisms activate. Refunds are proportional and may not equal your original investment due to sell taxes collected during the active period.

**Oracle and Infrastructure Risk.** The protocol depends on Switchboard VRF for epoch transitions. If the oracle service experiences downtime, epoch transitions stall. Swaps continue with stale tax rates during such periods and no funds are locked, but the protocol's core mechanics are degraded.

**Regulatory Risk.** The legal status of DeFi protocols, tokens, and related activities varies by jurisdiction and is subject to change. You are solely responsible for understanding and complying with all applicable laws, regulations, and tax obligations in your jurisdiction. The Doctor's legal team consists of a rubber duck and a very old copy of Black's Law Dictionary. Govern yourself accordingly.

**Blockchain Risk.** The protocol operates on the Solana blockchain. Solana network congestion, outages, hard forks, validator failures, or changes to the network's consensus mechanism could adversely affect the protocol's operation and your assets.
