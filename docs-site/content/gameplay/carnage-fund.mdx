# The Carnage Fund

> *"Every great experiment needs a controlled explosion. Controlled being a subjective term."*

---

## The Headline

The Carnage Fund is a protocol-controlled war chest that accumulates SOL from trading taxes and, at unpredictable intervals, **DETONATES**. Market-buying tokens and burning them into oblivion.

It is the single most dramatic mechanism in the Fantastical Finance Factory. It creates permanent deflation, massive arbitrage windows, and the kind of on-chain chaos that makes this protocol unlike anything else in DeFi.

---

## How It Gets Funded

**24% of all SOL taxes** flow directly into the Carnage Fund's SOL vault. Every taxed swap, every epoch, every day. The fund accumulates passively, growing larger with every trade.

The fund does not spend continuously. It waits. It accumulates. And then, when the VRF dice land on the right number, it strikes.

---

## When It Triggers

Carnage does not operate on a schedule. It is triggered by **verifiable randomness** during the epoch transition.

- **Probability per epoch:** ~4.3%
- **Expected frequency:** roughly **2 triggers per day** on mainnet (48 epochs x 4.3%)
- **Determination:** The VRF random value at each epoch transition decides whether Carnage fires

Some days it fires three times. Some days it fires once. Occasionally, it may not fire at all. That unpredictability is the point.

---

## What Happens When It Fires

When Carnage triggers, it follows one of three possible paths depending on whether the fund already holds tokens from a previous trigger:

### Path 1: Buy Only (the very first time it triggers)

When the fund has no tokens:
1. Take all SOL in the vault (up to 1,000 SOL maximum)
2. Market-buy either CRIME or FRAUD (50/50, determined by VRF)
3. Hold the purchased tokens until the next trigger

### Path 2: Burn Then Buy (~98% of triggers when holding tokens)

When the fund holds tokens from a previous buy:
1. **Burn ALL held tokens** -- permanently destroyed, removed from total supply forever
2. Market-buy CRIME or FRAUD with accumulated SOL (up to 1,000 SOL)
3. Hold the newly purchased tokens

This is the **primary deflationary path**. The vast majority of Carnage events burn tokens before buying more. Over time, this steadily reduces the circulating supply of CRIME and FRAUD.

### Path 3: Sell Then Buy (~2% of triggers when holding tokens)

On rare occasions, instead of burning:
1. **Sell all held tokens** back for SOL
2. Combine sale proceeds with accumulated SOL in the vault
3. Market-buy CRIME or FRAUD with the full amount (up to 1,000 SOL)
4. Hold the newly purchased tokens

This is the **rare chaos event**. Instead of burning, Carnage sells its holdings and immediately market-buys with the proceeds. If the sell and buy target different tokens, two pools get hit simultaneously and the arbitrage windows are enormous.

The buy target is VRF-determined and independent of what was sold. Carnage might sell CRIME and buy FRAUD, or sell CRIME and buy CRIME right back. Pure, calculated randomness.

> *"Oops."*

## MEV Protection

Carnage buys are protected against front-running bots through a two-phase timing model:

**Atomic execution** is the primary path. The Carnage buy executes in the same transaction as the VRF reveal, giving no time for MEV bots to see the result and front-run. The slippage floor is set tight at 85% of expected output.

**Fallback execution** opens after a short lock window (~20 seconds) if the atomic path was not included in the reveal transaction. Anyone can trigger the fallback, with a more lenient 75% slippage floor to prioritize execution.

**Expiration** can be called after ~2 minutes. If neither execution path succeeds, the SOL stays safely in the vault for the next trigger -- nothing is lost.

<Callout type="info">
Carnage swaps pay 0% protocol tax. They are subject only to the standard 1% LP fee. Taxing the protocol's own deflationary mechanism would be counterproductive -- the taxes generated by the arbitrage that Carnage creates far outweigh any tax collected from the Carnage swap itself.
</Callout>

## Permanent Deflation

When Carnage burns tokens, they are gone. Not locked, not frozen -- **destroyed**. The burn removes them from the total supply permanently. Mint authority is burned at launch, so no replacement tokens can ever be created.

Over months and years, this adds up. Every burn event permanently reduces the supply of CRIME and FRAUD, concentrating value among remaining holders.

---

## Massive Arbitrage Windows

A Carnage buy event can inject hundreds of SOL of buying pressure into a single pool in one transaction. This is not a gentle nudge -- it is a shock. The token's price spikes relative to its pair, the [soft peg](/gameplay/soft-peg) breaks hard, and arbitrageurs race to restore equilibrium.

These arbitrage events generate substantial tax revenue, which flows back to fund staking rewards and... right back into the Carnage Fund. The cycle feeds itself.

---

## The 1,000 SOL Cap

Each Carnage execution is capped at **1,000 SOL per swap**. If the fund has accumulated more than 1,000 SOL, only 1,000 SOL is used and the remainder stays in the vault for the next trigger.

This cap exists to bound pool impact, but it has an interesting side effect: during periods of extremely high trading volume, the Carnage Fund can grow faster than it detonates. This creates a growing stockpile that eventually fires off in multiple successive events, each one hitting the 1,000 SOL cap.

## On-Chain Statistics

The Carnage Fund tracks everything on-chain, permanently and verifiably:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Total SOL spent | Lifetime market-buy volume (monotonic) |
| Total CRIME burned | Lifetime CRIME destroyed |
| Total FRAUD burned | Lifetime FRAUD destroyed |
| Total triggers | Number of Carnage events executed |
| Current holdings | Which token and how much the fund holds now |
| Current SOL balance | How much SOL is in the vault waiting |

All of this is readable by anyone at any time. No hidden state, no off-chain tracking.
