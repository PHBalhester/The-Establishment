# Finding: H126 - No Anti-Flicker Protection on Route Selection

## Status: NOT VULNERABLE
## Confidence Score: 9

## Rationale
The hypothesis suggests the 10 BPS anti-flicker threshold masks meaningful route changes. Examining `app/hooks/useRoutes.ts:477-505`, the anti-flicker logic is well-implemented: (1) if the current selection no longer exists in new routes, it immediately switches to the best route (line 487), (2) if a different route beats the current by more than `FLICKER_THRESHOLD_BPS` (10 bps = 0.1%), it switches (line 497-499), (3) otherwise it keeps the current selection but updates its quote data via `matchingRoute` (line 504), meaning the displayed output amount always reflects fresh quotes. The 10 BPS threshold only prevents switching the *selected route label* -- the quote data (output amount, fees, price impact) is always up-to-date. For a 1 SOL swap, 10 BPS is 0.0001 SOL (100,000 lamports) -- a negligible difference that prevents distracting UI flickering.
