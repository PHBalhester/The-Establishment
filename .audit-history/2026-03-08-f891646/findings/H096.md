# H096 -- Conversion Vault: Reverse Conversion Rate

**Status:** SAFE
**Severity:** N/A (correctly implemented)
**Component:** `programs/conversion-vault/src/instructions/convert.rs`

## Hypothesis

Is the conversion direction logic correct? Could an attacker exploit a rate miscalculation or unsupported direction?

## Evidence

### Hardcoded rate (constants.rs line 7)
```
CONVERSION_RATE: u64 = 100
```

### Bidirectional support (convert.rs lines 101-113)
The `compute_output_with_mints` function handles exactly two directions:

1. **CRIME/FRAUD -> PROFIT**: `out = amount_in / 100` (integer division, remainder lost)
2. **PROFIT -> CRIME/FRAUD**: `out = amount_in.checked_mul(100)` (overflow-checked)

### Prohibited conversions
- **Same mint**: Rejected at line 99 (`SameMint` error)
- **CRIME <-> FRAUD direct**: Falls through to `InvalidMintPair` error at line 112 -- only CRIME<->PROFIT and FRAUD<->PROFIT are valid pairs
- **Zero input**: Rejected at line 98 (`ZeroAmount` error)
- **Dust (CRIME/FRAUD->PROFIT yields 0)**: Rejected at line 104 (`OutputTooSmall` error)
- **Overflow (PROFIT->CRIME/FRAUD)**: `checked_mul` catches u64 overflow at line 109

### Mint validation
The vault accounts are constrained with `token::mint = input_mint` and `token::mint = output_mint` (lines 38-50), ensuring the vaults match the declared mints. Mint addresses are resolved from compile-time constants (production) or stored VaultConfig (localnet).

### Rate symmetry check
100 CRIME -> 1 PROFIT -> 100 CRIME. The round-trip is lossless for multiples of 100. For non-multiples, the integer division in the CRIME->PROFIT direction loses the remainder (up to 99 tokens), which is a rounding-down-only pattern -- safe, slightly favors the vault.

## Conclusion

**No vulnerability.** The conversion rate is hardcoded, bidirectional with correct arithmetic direction, overflow-checked, and rejects all invalid mint pair combinations including direct CRIME<->FRAUD. The integer division rounding loss is inherent and documented.
