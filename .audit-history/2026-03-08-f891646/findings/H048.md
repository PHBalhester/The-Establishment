# H048: taxes_confirmed Unchecked by Tax Program

**Status:** CONFIRMED -- DESIGN CHOICE (INFORMATIONAL)
**Category:** Oracle / State Machine
**Severity:** LOW
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## Hypothesis

The Tax Program reads EpochState to get per-epoch tax rates but never checks the `taxes_confirmed` flag. During the VRF pending window (between `trigger_epoch_transition` and `consume_randomness`), swaps execute using the previous epoch's stale tax rates. Investigate whether this is intentional and assess economic impact.

---

### 4. VRF Pending Window Duration

**Normal case (oracle cooperates):** The VRF commit-reveal cycle is a two-TX sequence typically completing within 2-3 slots (~0.8-1.2 seconds at 400ms/slot). During this sub-second window, `taxes_confirmed = false` and swaps use the previous epoch's rates.

**Degraded case (oracle timeout):** If the Switchboard oracle fails to reveal, `taxes_confirmed` remains false for up to `VRF_TIMEOUT_SLOTS = 300` slots (~2 minutes). After timeout, `retry_epoch_vrf` starts a fresh 300-slot window. With infinite retries allowed, the protocol could theoretically remain in the `taxes_confirmed = false` state indefinitely if the oracle is persistently unavailable.

**Mainnet epoch length for context:** `SLOTS_PER_EPOCH = 4,500` (~30 minutes). Even a 300-slot VRF timeout is only ~6.7% of an epoch duration.

### 5. Economic Impact Analysis

**Can an attacker exploit the stale rates?**

The tax rates during the VRF pending window are the *previous* epoch's rates. These rates are:
- Already public on-chain (anyone can read EpochState)
- Already known to all market participants from the previous epoch
- Bounded to the same ranges as fresh rates: low {100-400} bps, high {1100-1400} bps

**Information asymmetry:** An attacker who monitors the Switchboard reveal TX in the mempool can see the *new* VRF bytes before `consume_randomness` lands. This creates a brief window where the attacker knows the new rates but the protocol still applies old rates. However:

1. The attacker cannot choose which rates to trade at -- old rates are the only option until `consume_randomness` completes.
2. The maximum tax rate difference between old and new is bounded: worst case is swapping from 1% to 14% (1,300 bps difference), saving at most 13% on a single trade.
3. The window is 1-2 slots (~0.4-0.8s) under normal conditions -- insufficient for meaningful volume.
4. During VRF timeout (300 slots), the attacker has no foreknowledge of new rates at all.

**Quantified worst case:** A trader executes a swap at 1% tax (old rate) when the new rate would be 14%. On a 10 SOL trade, they save 1.3 SOL in tax. This requires: (a) knowing the reveal will flip rates unfavorably, (b) landing the TX in the 1-2 slot window, (c) the swap itself being economically rational ignoring the tax savings. The MEV opportunity is marginal.

### 6. Design Rationale

The architecture document explicitly documents this as an intentional design choice:

**File:** `/Users/mlbob/Projects/Dr Fraudsworth/.audit/ARCHITECTURE.md` (line 146)
```
A-10 | taxes_confirmed intentionally unchecked by Tax | Design choice -- swaps use stale rates during VRF window
```

The design prioritizes **liveness over freshness**:
- Blocking swaps during VRF pending would create a DoS vector: anyone could grief the protocol by triggering epoch transitions during high-activity periods.
- VRF oracle failures would halt all trading until the oracle recovers (potentially 300+ slots per retry).
- The bounded tax rate range (1-14%) means stale rates are always within acceptable protocol parameters.

### 7. Alternative Design Consideration

If the Tax Program checked `taxes_confirmed`, it would need to either:
- **Reject swaps** when `taxes_confirmed = false` -- creates a liveness/DoS issue
- **Use a default/fallback rate** -- introduces new complexity and potential manipulation vectors
- **Use the genesis rate** -- not meaningfully different from using stale rates

The current approach (use previous epoch's rates) is the simplest and most robust option.

---

## Conclusion

**CONFIRMED as intentional design choice. Severity: LOW (Informational).**

The Tax Program deliberately omits a `taxes_confirmed` check to prioritize protocol liveness. Swaps continue with the previous epoch's bounded tax rates during the brief VRF pending window. This is documented in the architecture (A-10), the mirror struct comments explicitly note that VRF fields are "not used" by the Tax Program, and the economic impact is negligible under normal operation.

**Risk factors:**
- Normal window: ~1-2 slots (0.4-0.8s) -- effectively zero economic impact
- Degraded window: 300 slots (~2 min) on oracle timeout -- protocol remains functional with previous rates, no foreknowledge attack possible
- Worst case: indefinite oracle failure -- protocol operates at stale but bounded rates, which is strictly better than halting all swaps

**No remediation recommended.** The current behavior correctly prioritizes availability over rate freshness. The bounded tax rate range (1-14%) ensures that stale rates are never economically catastrophic.
