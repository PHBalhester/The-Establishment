# H031 -- Dual-Curve Grief Attack

**Auditor:** Hypothesis Investigator (sos-002)
**Category:** State Machine, Timing
**Origin:** Novel (ARCHITECTURE.md Section 9, Item 4; Timing context "Dual-curve deadline interaction")
**Status:** PARTIALLY VALID -- grief vector exists but is economically constrained
**Severity:** LOW (downgraded from hypothesized MEDIUM-HIGH)
**Confidence:** HIGH

---

## Hypothesis

> Strategically prevent one curve from filling (sell near deadline) to force both curves into refund mode. Costs only gas.

## Target Code

- `programs/bonding_curve/src/instructions/sell.rs` -- bonding curve sell handler
- `programs/bonding_curve/src/instructions/mark_failed.rs` -- permissionless failure marking
- `programs/bonding_curve/src/instructions/prepare_transition.rs` -- requires BOTH curves Filled
- `programs/bonding_curve/src/instructions/purchase.rs` -- wallet cap enforcement
- `programs/bonding_curve/src/state.rs:137-143` -- `is_refund_eligible()` coupling logic
- `programs/bonding_curve/src/constants.rs` -- TARGET_TOKENS, MAX_TOKENS_PER_WALLET, SELL_TAX_BPS

## Investigation

### 1. Are the two curves independent or coupled?

**Coupled at graduation; independent at failure.** Each curve (CRIME and FRAUD) has its own independent `CurveState` PDA with independent `deadline_slot`, `tokens_sold`, and `status`. However, `prepare_transition` (line 53-78) requires BOTH `crime_curve_state.status == Filled` AND `fraud_curve_state.status == Filled` to graduate either curve. If one curve fails, the other -- even if Filled -- cannot graduate and becomes refund-eligible via `is_refund_eligible(partner_status=Failed)` at `state.rs:140`.

**Verdict: One curve failing forces the other into refund mode.** This is the coupling that makes the grief vector possible.

### 2. Does one failing force the other to fail?

Not directly. The Filled curve does not transition to `Failed` status. Instead, it remains `Filled` but becomes refund-eligible through the compound state check. The `claim_refund` and `consolidate_for_refund` instructions accept a `partner_curve_state` account and use `is_refund_eligible(partner.status)` to gate access. So the partner curve staying `Filled` while the griefed curve is `Failed` still triggers refund mode for both.

### 3. Attack scenario and cost analysis

**Phase 1 -- Buy tokens on target curve:**
The attacker buys tokens on the curve they want to grief (say FRAUD). With `MAX_TOKENS_PER_WALLET = 20M` (20,000,000,000,000 base units) and `TARGET_TOKENS = 460M` (460,000,000,000,000 base units), each wallet can hold ~4.35% of the target supply.

**Phase 2 -- Wait until near deadline:**
The attacker waits until `clock.slot` approaches `curve.deadline_slot`. Purchases are blocked at `deadline_slot` (hard cutoff at `purchase.rs:108-111`), and `mark_failed` becomes callable at `deadline_slot + FAILURE_GRACE_SLOTS (150)` (`mark_failed.rs:56-63`).

**Phase 3 -- Sell near deadline:**
The attacker sells their tokens back to the curve just before the deadline, driving `tokens_sold` below `TARGET_TOKENS`. After the deadline passes, no new purchases can fill the gap, and the curve is marked Failed permissionlessly.

### 4. Economic cost to the attacker

This is where the hypothesis breaks down. **The attack does NOT cost "only gas":**

**a) 15% sell tax is non-refundable:**
`sell.rs:174-179` applies a 15% tax (SELL_TAX_BPS = 1500) on gross SOL proceeds, routed to the tax escrow. The attacker loses 15% of the SOL value of the tokens they sell. For a meaningful grief (selling enough to prevent filling), this is substantial.

**b) Bonding curve price mechanics penalize late sellers:**
The linear price curve `P(x) = P_START + (P_END - P_START) * x / TOTAL_FOR_SALE` means tokens bought later (at higher `tokens_sold`) cost more. Selling returns the integral value at the current position minus 15% tax. If other users sell in response, the price drops further, amplifying the attacker's loss.

**c) Quantitative example:**
- Suppose the FRAUD curve is at 440M/460M tokens sold (95.6% filled), near deadline.
- The attacker holds 20M tokens (one wallet) bought near the top of the curve.
- Cost to buy 20M tokens near position 440M: approximately `calculate_sol_for_tokens(440M_base, 20M_base)`. Given the linear curve, price at 440M is roughly `900 + 2550 * 440/460 = 3338 lamports/human_token`. For 20M human tokens: ~66.8 SOL.
- Selling those 20M tokens: gross return ~66.8 SOL, tax = 15% = ~10.0 SOL lost.
- The attacker loses ~10 SOL to push tokens_sold from 460M back to 440M.
- But 440M is still far from preventing the fill. To meaningfully grief, the attacker needs to sell enough that remaining participants cannot fill it in time.

**d) Multi-wallet amplification:**
The attacker could use multiple wallets. To buy 100M tokens (to create a 100M gap when sold), they need 5 wallets and ~217 SOL (at average prices near the top). The 15% sell tax costs them ~32.5 SOL. This is a non-trivial economic loss for the attacker.

**e) Other participants can re-purchase:**
After the attacker sells, the price drops, making tokens cheaper. Legitimate buyers can re-purchase the dip before the deadline. The attacker's sell creates a buying opportunity.

### 5. Timing constraints

- `DEADLINE_SLOTS = 432,000` (~48 hours). The attacker must time sells very close to the deadline to minimize the window for other buyers to react.
- Purchases and sells share the same `deadline_slot` cutoff (`sell.rs:142-145`, `purchase.rs:108-111`). There is no asymmetry -- the attacker cannot sell after purchases are blocked.
- After deadline, there is a 150-slot (~60 second) grace period before `mark_failed` is callable. During this grace period, neither purchases nor sells can execute. No last-second re-fill is possible.

### 6. Can the attacker avoid the 15% tax?

No. The sell tax is computed on gross SOL output (`sell.rs:174-179`) and routed to the tax escrow before the user receives anything. There is no exempt sell path on the bonding curve.

### 7. Can the attacker use Token-2022 transfers to move tokens to a non-curve recipient?

No. Selling back to the curve is the only way to reduce `tokens_sold`. Transferring tokens between wallets does not affect `tokens_sold`. The attacker must use the `sell` instruction, which always applies the 15% tax.

## Verdict

**PARTIALLY VALID -- the coupling mechanism is real but the economics are punitive.**

The dual-curve coupling at `prepare_transition` (requiring both curves Filled for graduation) does create a grief vector where preventing one curve from filling forces both into refund mode. However:

1. **The attack costs significantly more than "only gas."** The 15% sell tax imposes a meaningful cost proportional to the grief magnitude. Selling 100M tokens to create a gap costs ~32.5 SOL in non-recoverable tax.
2. **Other participants can counter the grief** by re-purchasing during the price dip.
3. **The attacker must also front the capital** to buy tokens in the first place (hundreds of SOL for a meaningful position).
4. **Timing is constrained** -- purchases and sells share the same deadline, so the attacker cannot sell after purchases are blocked.

The attack would only succeed against a curve that is already struggling to fill organically (say, stalled at 80-90% with hours remaining). In that scenario, the attacker's sells tip a borderline situation into failure. Against a healthy launch with strong demand, the 48-hour window and price-dip buying opportunity make this grief impractical.

**Severity downgraded to LOW** because the economic cost to the attacker is substantial and scales with the magnitude of the grief.

## Recommendations

1. **Consider independent graduation paths (design change):** Allow each curve to graduate independently if it fills, even if the partner hasn't. This eliminates the coupling entirely but changes the protocol's economic design (the dual-launch is an intentional feature).

2. **Add a fill-progress monitoring alert:** Off-chain monitoring that alerts the team if either curve's `tokens_sold` drops significantly near the deadline, allowing social-layer response.

3. **No on-chain fix required for current severity level.** The 15% sell tax and buying opportunity for other participants provide sufficient economic defense against this grief vector.
