# H011: PROFIT Routing Reduces Effective Tax Below Design Intent

**Strategy:** STR-011
**Determination:** CONFIRMED
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Summary

The PROFIT pool routing path (SOL → CRIME/FRAUD → PROFIT → CRIME/FRAUD → SOL) is completely untaxed by the Tax Program, creating a cross-faction arbitrage route that reduces effective taxation from the intended ~15% round-trip to ~5% when combined with asymmetric tax rates. This is by design per the economic model, but represents a significant (33-67%) reduction in protocol tax revenue that directly impacts staking yield.

```
**FINDING ECON-02 (MEDIUM)**: The PROFIT routing path creates a tax arbitrage opportunity. Consider:

1. User buys CRIME with SOL (pays buy tax, e.g., 2%)
2. User swaps CRIME -> PROFIT (untaxed, 0.5% LP fee)
3. User swaps PROFIT -> FRAUD (untaxed, 0.5% LP fee)
4. User sells FRAUD for SOL (pays sell tax, e.g., 2%)

Compared to directly buying and selling the same token (4% round-trip for cheap side), this cross-faction

- If CRIME is "cheap" (low buy tax 1-4%, high sell tax 11-14%) and FRAUD is "expensive" (high buy tax
  11-14%, low sell tax 1-4%):
- Buy CRIME cheaply (2% tax) -> swap to PROFIT (0.5%) -> swap to FRAUD (0.5%) -> Sell FRAUD cheaply (2% tax)
- Total cost: 2% + 0.5% + 0.5% + 2% = **5%** round-trip
- Direct buy CRIME then sell CRIME: 2% + 13% = **15%** round-trip
- Direct buy FRAUD then sell FRAUD: 13% + 2% = **15%** round-trip

**Impact**: The PROFIT bridge reduces the effective tax by routing through the cross-faction path.
```

The economic analysis confirms this is a known, significant arbitrage path.

## Root Cause

The protocol's three-pool design (CRIME/SOL, FRAUD/SOL, CRIME/PROFIT, FRAUD/PROFIT) creates a routing graph where PROFIT acts as a cross-faction bridge. The design decision to make PROFIT swaps **untaxed** (per Tax_Pool_Logic_Spec.md) means users can route around the asymmetric tax model by:

1. Entering through the cheap side (low buy tax)
2. Routing through PROFIT (untaxed) to switch factions
3. Exiting through the cheap side (low sell tax)

This bypasses the protocol's intended penalty for "round-tripping" (buying and selling the same faction token), which would incur high tax on one direction.

## Attack Scenario

**Scenario:** Sophisticated trader exploiting asymmetric tax rates during epoch transition.

### Setup
- Epoch N: CRIME is cheap (2% buy, 13% sell), FRAUD is expensive (13% buy, 2% sell)
- VRF reveals epoch N+1 will flip: CRIME expensive, FRAUD cheap
- Trader has capital in SOL

### Execution Path

**Step 1: Buy CRIME cheaply**
- Input: 100 SOL
- Tax: 100 * 0.02 = 2 SOL (tax collected)
- Net to AMM: 98 SOL
- AMM fee: 98 * 0.01 = 0.98 SOL
- Receive: ~97 SOL worth of CRIME

**Step 2: Swap CRIME → PROFIT (untaxed)**
- Input: 97 SOL worth of CRIME
- Tax: 0 (no tax on PROFIT swaps)
- AMM fee: 97 * 0.005 = 0.485 SOL equivalent
- Receive: ~96.5 SOL worth of PROFIT

**Step 3: Swap PROFIT → FRAUD (untaxed)**
- Input: 96.5 SOL worth of PROFIT
- Tax: 0 (no tax on PROFIT swaps)
- AMM fee: 96.5 * 0.005 = 0.48 SOL equivalent
- Receive: ~96 SOL worth of FRAUD

**Step 4: Sell FRAUD cheaply**
- Input: 96 SOL worth of FRAUD
- Gross output: ~96 SOL
- Tax: 96 * 0.02 = 1.92 SOL (tax collected)
- Net received: ~94.08 SOL

### Result
- **Protocol tax collected:** 2 + 1.92 = 3.92 SOL (3.92% of original input)
- **User net cost:** 100 - 94.08 = 5.92 SOL (5.92% total including fees)
- **If direct CRIME round-trip:** 2% + 13% = 15% tax alone, plus 2% in LP fees = ~17% total
- **Tax revenue reduction:** 15% → 3.92% = **73.9% less tax revenue**

### Impact Multiplier

If 50% of protocol volume routes through PROFIT to exploit this arbitrage:
- Expected tax revenue at $1M volume: $150,000 (15% effective tax)
- Actual tax revenue with 50% PROFIT routing: ~$75,000
- **Direct impact on staking yield:** 75% of tax goes to stakers, so staking APY reduced by ~50%

## Impact Assessment

**Severity: MEDIUM** (not HIGH) because:

1. **Intentional Design:** The economic model document explicitly acknowledges this pattern as "likely intentional by design (PROFIT has utility as a routing token)."

2. **Market Forces:** The arbitrage only produces meaningful savings when tax rates are asymmetric across factions. When both factions are at similar tax rates (e.g., both 7%), routing through PROFIT saves minimal tax.

3. **Liquidity Constraints:** PROFIT pools need sufficient depth to support large volume without excessive slippage. Thin PROFIT pools self-limit the arbitrage.

4. **Multi-Hop Slippage:** Two additional swaps (4 total vs 2 for direct path) means 4x the price impact and 4x the MEV sandwich risk, which acts as a natural brake on arbitrage volume.

**However**, the impact is still significant:

- **Tax revenue loss:** Up to 73% reduction for cross-faction routing volume
- **Staking yield impact:** Direct 1:1 reduction in SOL yield to stakers (75% of tax)
- **Economic model undermining:** The asymmetric tax design (cheap vs expensive side) loses its purpose if users can route around it
- **Faction balance disruption:** The intended economic pressure keeping CRIME/FRAUD balanced is weakened

## Remediation

### Option 1: Add Tax to PROFIT Pool Swaps (Breaking Change)
**Impact:** Changes core economic model

```rust
// In swap_profit_buy.rs and swap_profit_sell.rs, add:
let tax_bps = /* decide on rate: 1-5% reasonable */;
let tax_amount = calculate_tax(amount_in, tax_bps)?;
let (staking, carnage, treasury) = split_distribution(tax_amount)?;
// ... distribute tax before AMM CPI
```

**Pros:**
- Closes arbitrage completely
- Increases protocol tax revenue
- Restores asymmetric tax model effectiveness

**Cons:**
- Removes PROFIT's routing utility (may be intended feature)
- Reduces appeal of PROFIT token (less utility = less demand)
- May discourage staking if acquiring PROFIT costs more

### Option 2: Increase PROFIT Pool LP Fees (Non-Breaking)
**Impact:** Minimal code change, economic adjustment

```rust
// In amm/src/constants.rs:
pub const PROFIT_POOL_FEE_BPS: u16 = 200; // 2% instead of 0.5%
```

**Pros:**
- No protocol logic changes
- Makes routing less attractive (2% + 2% = 4% fee overhead)
- LP fee revenue still goes to protocol (indirectly via future mechanisms)

**Cons:**
- Doesn't fully close arbitrage (still saves tax vs direct route)
- Makes PROFIT less attractive for legitimate staking flow

### Option 3: Accept as Intended Design (No Change)
**Impact:** Document and monitor

**Rationale:**
- Economic analysis states this is "likely intentional by design"
- PROFIT routing serves a purpose: cross-faction liquidity bridge
- Natural market forces (slippage, MEV, multi-hop complexity) limit abuse
- Asymmetric tax model still applies to entry/exit, just not the middle routing

**Recommendation:**
- Document this clearly in user-facing materials
- Monitor on-chain data for PROFIT routing volume %
- Adjust if abuse exceeds 30% of total volume

### Option 4: Hybrid — Tiered PROFIT Tax (Sophisticated)
**Impact:** Adds complexity but preserves utility

```rust
// Tax PROFIT swaps only when combined with SOL swaps in same TX
// - CRIME → PROFIT alone: 0% tax (legitimate staking flow)
// - SOL → CRIME → PROFIT → FRAUD → SOL (detected via TX analysis): apply tax
```

**Pros:**
- Preserves PROFIT utility for stakers
- Penalizes cross-faction arbitrage specifically

**Cons:**
- Complex to implement (requires TX-level state tracking)
- Bypassable (split into separate TXs)
- Not worth the engineering complexity

**Recommended Action:** Accept as **intended design** with monitoring. The economic analysis explicitly acknowledges this as likely intentional. The protocol's goal is a faction-based memecoin/yield farm, not maximum tax revenue. PROFIT routing serves legitimate purposes:

1. **Cross-faction liquidity:** Allows users to switch factions without selling to SOL
2. **Staking incentive:** Easy CRIME/FRAUD → PROFIT flow encourages staking
3. **Market efficiency:** Three-pool triangle enables price discovery

However, if post-launch metrics show >40% of volume routing through PROFIT to avoid tax, consider **Option 2** (raise PROFIT pool fees to 1.5-2%) as a non-breaking adjustment.
