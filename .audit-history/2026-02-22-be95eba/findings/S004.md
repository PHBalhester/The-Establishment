# S004: Triangle Arbitrage Destabilizes Pool Ratios

**Strategy:** S004
**Determination:** CONFIRMED (DUPLICATE OF H011)
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Summary

The protocol's four-pool AMM structure (SOL/CRIME, SOL/FRAUD, CRIME/PROFIT, FRAUD/PROFIT) forms a triangle arbitrage graph. When prices diverge across the triangle, arbitrageurs can extract value through multi-hop routing. The zero-tax PROFIT path makes this arbitrage profitable at smaller divergences than would occur in a traditional multi-pool AMM, creating continuous pressure that destabilizes the intended price relationships between pools.

**This finding duplicates H011 (PROFIT Routing Reduces Effective Tax Below Design Intent)** which comprehensively analyzed the same economic vector. H011 focused on tax revenue reduction; S004 examines the pool ratio destabilization aspect of the same underlying mechanism.

### 3. Constant-Product Formula Creates Arbitrage Opportunities

**File:** `/Users/mlbob/Projects/Dr Fraudsworth/programs/amm/src/helpers/math.rs` (lines 58-76)
```rust
pub fn calculate_swap_output(
    reserve_in: u64,
    reserve_out: u64,
    effective_input: u128,
) -> Option<u64> {
    let r_in = reserve_in as u128;
    let r_out = reserve_out as u128;

    let numerator = r_out.checked_mul(effective_input)?;
    let denominator = r_in.checked_add(effective_input)?;

    if denominator == 0 {
        return None;
    }

    let output = numerator.checked_div(denominator)?;
    u64::try_from(output).ok()
}
```

Each pool maintains its own independent `k = reserve_a * reserve_b` invariant. When external trades or Carnage operations shift reserves in one pool, the other pools do not automatically rebalance, creating temporary price divergences.

## Triangle Arbitrage Mechanics

### Traditional Multi-Pool Arbitrage

In a typical AMM with multiple pools (e.g., Uniswap V2 style), arbitrage between pools A-B, B-C, and A-C is profitable when:

```
Price divergence > (fee_A + fee_B + fee_C)
```

For standard 0.3% fees: divergence must exceed ~0.9% to be profitable.

### Dr Fraudsworth Triangle Arbitrage

The protocol's structure creates **two classes** of arbitrage loops:

#### Class 1: SOL-Mediated Triangle (Taxed)
```
CRIME → SOL → FRAUD → SOL → CRIME
Costs: 2 × (1-14% tax + 1% LP fee) = 4-30% round-trip
```
This is NOT profitable due to high taxation. Protocol tax acts as arbitrage resistance.

#### Class 2: PROFIT-Mediated Triangle (Partially Untaxed)
```
SOL → CRIME → PROFIT → FRAUD → SOL
Costs: 2% buy tax + 0.5% LP fee + 0.5% LP fee + 2% sell tax = 5% total
```

vs direct route:
```
SOL → CRIME → SOL (round-trip)
Costs: 2% buy tax + 13% sell tax + 2% LP fees = 17% total
```

**Arbitrage profitability threshold:** Price divergence between pools only needs to exceed ~5% (vs 17% for direct route) to make PROFIT routing profitable.

### Impact on Pool Ratios

When price relationships drift between pools:

1. **SOL/CRIME pool:** CRIME trades at 1.00 SOL per token
2. **CRIME/PROFIT pool:** CRIME trades at 10 PROFIT per token
3. **PROFIT/FRAUD pool:** 10 PROFIT trades at 1 FRAUD
4. **SOL/FRAUD pool:** FRAUD trades at 1.05 SOL per token

**Implied price:** CRIME → PROFIT → FRAUD → SOL yields 1.05 SOL (5% profit before fees)

**Arbitrage path:**
- Buy 100 SOL worth of CRIME (pay 2% tax + 1% fee = 97 SOL enters pool)
- Receive ~97 CRIME
- Swap 97 CRIME → PROFIT (0.5% fee = 96.5 PROFIT)
- Swap 96.5 PROFIT → FRAUD (0.5% fee = 96 FRAUD)
- Sell 96 FRAUD → SOL (pay 2% tax = net 94 SOL)
- Gross output: 96 FRAUD × 1.05 SOL/FRAUD = ~100.8 SOL
- Net after tax: 100.8 × 0.98 = 98.8 SOL
- **Profit: 98.8 - 100 = -1.2 SOL (loss)**

**BUT** if FRAUD trades at 1.10 SOL (10% divergence):
- Gross: 96 × 1.10 = 105.6 SOL
- Net: 105.6 × 0.98 = 103.5 SOL
- **Profit: 3.5 SOL (3.5% profit)**

**Conclusion:** PROFIT routing becomes profitable at **~6% price divergence** between SOL/CRIME and SOL/FRAUD pools, compared to ~15% needed for direct arbitrage.

### Continuous Rebalancing Pressure

Every ~30 minutes (mainnet epoch length), the VRF flips tax rates:
- CRIME cheap → CRIME expensive (1-4% buy → 11-14% buy)
- FRAUD expensive → FRAUD cheap (11-14% buy → 1-4% buy)

This creates **predictable directional pressure:**

1. **Epoch N (CRIME cheap):** Users preferentially buy CRIME
2. **CRIME/SOL pool:** CRIME reserve decreases, SOL reserve increases
3. **Epoch N+1 (FRAUD cheap):** Users switch to buying FRAUD
4. **FRAUD/SOL pool:** FRAUD reserve decreases, SOL reserve increases
5. **Cross-pool arbitrage:** PROFIT bridge enables arbitrageurs to profit from the lag between epoch flip and pool rebalancing

**Result:** Pool ratios are continuously destabilized by:
- VRF-driven demand shifts (predictable)
- Carnage operations (4.3% of epochs, random target)
- PROFIT routing arbitrage (opportunistic)

## Quantified Impact

### Scenario: Post-Carnage Arbitrage

**Setup:**
- Carnage buys 1000 SOL worth of CRIME and burns it (98% probability)
- CRIME/SOL pool: CRIME reserve reduced by ~1000 tokens
- CRIME price in SOL pool: increases ~10% (assuming moderate liquidity)
- FRAUD price unchanged initially

**Arbitrage window:**
- SOL/CRIME: 1 CRIME = 1.10 SOL (inflated post-Carnage)
- SOL/FRAUD: 1 FRAUD = 1.00 SOL (unchanged)
- PROFIT bridge: 1 CRIME = 10 PROFIT = 1 FRAUD (pre-arbitrage rate)

**Arbitrageur actions:**
1. Buy FRAUD with SOL at 1.00 (cheap side)
2. FRAUD → PROFIT → CRIME via untaxed route
3. Sell CRIME at 1.10 SOL (expensive side)
4. **Profit:** ~10% - 5% (fees/tax) = **~5% profit**

**Pool impact:**
- CRIME/SOL pool: Sell pressure reduces CRIME price back toward 1.00
- FRAUD/SOL pool: Buy pressure increases FRAUD price toward 1.10
- **Equilibrium restored** but at the cost of ~5% value extraction from pool reserves

**Per-epoch value leak:** If Carnage triggers 4.3% of epochs and each creates a 5% arbitrage opportunity on ~1000 SOL volume:
- Value extracted per epoch: 1000 × 0.05 = 50 SOL per arbitrage
- Expected loss per epoch: 50 × 0.043 = **2.15 SOL/epoch**
- Monthly (1440 epochs): **~3,100 SOL/month** leaked to arbitrageurs

This value comes from protocol-owned liquidity reserves, permanently reducing the protocol's ability to provide stable pricing.

## Why This Differs from Intended Design

The economic model (ECON-02) acknowledges PROFIT routing as "likely intentional by design" for:
1. Cross-faction liquidity bridge
2. Staking incentive (easy CRIME/FRAUD → PROFIT conversion)
3. Market efficiency via three-pool price discovery

**However**, the tax arbitrage focus in H011 did not fully address the **pool ratio destabilization** aspect:

- **H011 impact:** Reduced tax revenue (33-67% less protocol income)
- **S004 impact:** Pool reserves leak value to arbitrageurs, reducing protocol-owned liquidity depth over time

These are **distinct but related** impacts of the same architectural choice.

## Attack Scenario

**Adversary:** Sophisticated MEV bot with capital and pool state monitoring

**Goal:** Extract maximum value from post-Carnage price divergences

**Execution:**
1. Monitor `EpochState.carnage_pending` flag on-chain
2. When `carnage_pending = true`, observe VRF byte 7 to determine Carnage target (CRIME or FRAUD)
3. If target = CRIME:
   - Pre-position: Buy FRAUD cheaply before Carnage executes
   - Wait for Carnage to buy/burn CRIME (inflates CRIME price in SOL pool)
   - Execute arbitrage: FRAUD → PROFIT → CRIME → SOL
   - Profit from price divergence between pools
4. Repeat every Carnage event (~4.3% of epochs = ~2x per day)

**Expected profit:** 2-5% per arbitrage opportunity on 500-1000 SOL volume = **10-50 SOL per event**

**Protocol cost:** Value extracted from pool reserves, plus increased slippage for legitimate users during rebalancing period

## Remediation

### Option 1: Accept as Intended Design (RECOMMENDED)

**Rationale from H011:**
- Economic analysis states PROFIT routing is "likely intentional by design"
- Natural market forces (slippage, multi-hop complexity, MEV risk) limit abuse
- Asymmetric tax model still applies to entry/exit points

**Additional rationale for S004:**
- Triangle arbitrage serves a **legitimate price discovery** function
- Arbitrageurs provide **liquidity rebalancing** after Carnage events
- Without arbitrage, pools would remain inefficiently priced for longer periods
- The 5-6% profitability threshold is relatively high (not HFT-level MEV)

**Action:** Document and monitor. If post-launch metrics show >40% of volume routing through PROFIT specifically for arbitrage (not staking), consider Option 2.

### Option 2: Increase PROFIT Pool LP Fees

**Change:** Raise PROFIT pool fees from 0.5% to 1.5-2.0%

**File:** `/Users/mlbob/Projects/Dr Fraudsworth/programs/amm/src/constants.rs`
```rust
pub const PROFIT_POOL_FEE_BPS: u16 = 200; // 2.0% instead of 0.5%
```

**Impact:**
- Arbitrage cost: 5% → 8% (2% + 2% + 2% + 2% instead of 5%)
- Profitability threshold: ~6% divergence → ~9% divergence
- Reduces arbitrage frequency without eliminating it
- Preserves PROFIT utility for staking (one-way CRIME/FRAUD → PROFIT still has reasonable cost)

**Pros:**
- Non-breaking change (just a constant update)
- Reduces value extraction from pools
- Still allows market efficiency via arbitrage at larger divergences

**Cons:**
- Makes PROFIT acquisition more expensive for legitimate stakers
- May reduce PROFIT demand if staking flow becomes costly

### Option 3: Add Tax to PROFIT Swaps (NOT RECOMMENDED)

This would fully close the arbitrage but fundamentally change PROFIT's role:
- Removes cross-faction bridge utility
- Increases complexity (tax calculation on all paths)
- Contradicts documented design intent per Tax_Pool_Logic_Spec.md
