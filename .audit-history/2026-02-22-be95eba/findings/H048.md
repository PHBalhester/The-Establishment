# H048: Cross-Faction Arbitrage via Simultaneous Pool Imbalance

**Strategy:** STR-048
**Determination:** DUPLICATE OF H011
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Confidence:** HIGH

## Summary

This finding is a **duplicate** of H011 (PROFIT Routing Reduces Effective Tax Below Design Intent). Both findings describe the same fundamental vulnerability: using PROFIT pools as an untaxed routing bridge to exploit asymmetric tax rates between CRIME and FRAUD factions. While STR-048 frames this as "cross-faction arbitrage via pool imbalance," the root cause, attack path, and economic impact are identical to H011's "PROFIT routing tax arbitrage."

3. PROFIT → FRAUD (0% tax + 0.5% LP fee)  ← untaxed routing

These are the **exact same** 4-hop routing strategy.

### 3. Economic Impact is Identical

**STR-048 Hypothesis:**
> If CRIME/SOL and FRAUD/SOL pools are at different prices (different market caps), buying the cheaper faction with SOL, routing through PROFIT, and selling as the expensive faction extracts risk-free profit after taxes.

**H011 Analysis (lines 160-175):**
> If CRIME is "cheap" (low buy tax 1-4%, high sell tax 11-14%) and FRAUD is "expensive" (high buy tax 11-14%, low sell tax 1-4%):
> - Buy CRIME cheaply (2% tax) → swap to PROFIT (0.5%) → swap to FRAUD (0.5%) → Sell FRAUD cheaply (2% tax)
> - Total cost: 2% + 0.5% + 0.5% + 2% = **5%** round-trip
> - Direct buy CRIME then sell CRIME: 2% + 13% = **15%** round-trip

Both findings describe extracting value by:
1. Entering on the cheap side (low buy tax)
2. Routing through untaxed PROFIT pools
3. Exiting on the cheap side (low sell tax)
4. Achieving ~5% total cost vs ~15% direct round-trip

## Calculation: Total Friction for Cross-Faction Arbitrage

Let's verify the hypothesis from STR-048:

> Calculate: for arbitrage to be profitable, the price discrepancy must exceed total friction

**Minimum Friction (when both factions are at low tax rates):**
- SOL → CRIME: 3% buy tax + 1% LP fee = 4%
- CRIME → PROFIT: 0% tax + 0.5% LP fee = 0.5%
- PROFIT → FRAUD: 0% tax + 0.5% LP fee = 0.5%
- FRAUD → SOL: 3% sell tax + 1% LP fee = 4%
- **Total minimum friction: 9%**

**Maximum Tax Savings (when asymmetry is highest):**
- Best case PROFIT route: 3% + 0.5% + 0.5% + 3% = **7% total cost**
- Worst case direct route: 3% buy + 14% sell = **17% tax alone** (plus 2% LP fees = 19%)
- **Tax savings: 19% - 7% = 12 percentage points**

This confirms STR-048's hypothesis: when the asymmetry is large (cheap vs expensive side), arbitrage is profitable despite friction. However, this is **exactly the calculation H011 performed** (lines 127-131):

```
Protocol tax collected: 3.92 SOL (3.92% of original input)
User net cost: 5.92 SOL (5.92% total including fees)
If direct CRIME round-trip: 2% + 13% = 15% tax alone, plus 2% in LP fees = ~17% total
Tax revenue reduction: 15% → 3.92% = 73.9% less tax revenue
```

## Difference Analysis: STR-048 vs H011

**STR-048 frames this as:**
> "Cross-Faction Arbitrage via Simultaneous Pool Imbalance"
> "If CRIME/SOL and FRAUD/SOL pools are at different prices (different market caps)..."

**H011 frames this as:**
> "PROFIT Routing Reduces Effective Tax Below Design Intent"
> "If CRIME is 'cheap' (low buy tax, high sell tax) and FRAUD is 'expensive'..."

**Analysis:**
- STR-048 emphasizes "pool imbalance" and "different prices/market caps"
- H011 emphasizes "asymmetric tax rates" (cheap side vs expensive side)

These are **not different vulnerabilities**. The asymmetric tax rates (cheap vs expensive) ARE the source of pool imbalance and price differences. When VRF assigns CRIME as cheap and FRAUD as expensive:
- CRIME has lower buy tax → more buying pressure → CRIME/SOL pool gets CRIME-heavy
- FRAUD has higher buy tax → less buying pressure → FRAUD/SOL pool gets SOL-heavy
- This creates the "simultaneous pool imbalance" STR-048 describes

The pool imbalance is a **symptom** of the asymmetric tax design. The **exploit** is PROFIT routing. Both findings target the same root cause.
