# S010: Conversion Vault PROFIT Drain via Arbitrage Loop

**Status:** CONFIRMED
**Severity:** LOW
**Category:** Economic, Design
**Inspired by:** H008, H025

## Summary

The conversion vault exchanges CRIME/FRAUD for PROFIT at a fixed 100:1 rate with no rate limit, no cooldown, and no oracle. H025 previously investigated and classified this as "Intended Design with Bounded, Acceptable Risk" at LOW severity. This finding re-examines the compound scenario specifically: if PROFIT trades below the 100:1 implied vault rate AND the protocol is in a low-tax epoch, a bidirectional arbitrage loop (buy cheap PROFIT on AMM → convert to CRIME/FRAUD at vault rate → sell CRIME/FRAUD on AMM) can drain vault PROFIT. The question is whether the tax friction is genuinely sufficient to prevent profitable grinding and whether any edge conditions exist that H025 did not fully model.

## Analysis

### Vault Configuration and Conversion Math

From `convert.rs:101-113`:
```rust
if (*input_mint == *crime || *input_mint == *fraud) && *output_mint == *profit {
    let out = amount_in / CONVERSION_RATE;  // CONVERSION_RATE = 100
    require!(out > 0, VaultError::OutputTooSmall);
    Ok(out)
} else if *input_mint == *profit && (*output_mint == *crime || *output_mint == *fraud) {
    amount_in.checked_mul(CONVERSION_RATE).ok_or_else(|| error!(VaultError::MathOverflow))
}
```

Seeding from `initialize.ts`:
- `vault_crime`: 250,000,000 CRIME
- `vault_fraud`: 250,000,000 FRAUD
- `vault_profit`: 19,999,999 PROFIT (all of supply minus 1 dead stake)

The vault is seeded once. No replenishment mechanism exists on-chain.

### Arbitrage Loop Construction

**Loop Direction A (PROFIT undervalued vs. 100:1 implied rate):**

The 100:1 rate means: 1 PROFIT = 100 CRIME/FRAUD in vault terms. If the AMM market price is below this (i.e., 1 PROFIT buys fewer than 100 CRIME in the AMM), an arbitrageur can:

1. Buy PROFIT cheaply on AMM (AMM rate: 1 PROFIT = 80 CRIME equivalent)
2. Convert PROFIT → CRIME at vault (100 CRIME per PROFIT)
3. Sell CRIME on AMM at prevailing rate
4. Net: bought 80 CRIME worth of PROFIT, got 100 CRIME out = 20 CRIME profit per cycle (before taxes)

**Loop Direction B (CRIME/FRAUD undervalued vs. PROFIT):**

If the AMM market price implies 1 PROFIT buys more than 100 CRIME:

1. Buy CRIME cheaply on AMM
2. Convert 100 CRIME → 1 PROFIT at vault
3. Sell PROFIT on AMM for more than the CRIME purchase cost
4. Net: arbitrage profits from vault's fixed rate acting as a price ceiling

### Tax Friction Analysis (Extended from H025)

H025 analyzed friction as approximately 8% minimum. This analysis examines epoch-specific cases:

| Epoch Tax State | Buy Tax | Sell Tax | AMM Fee | Round-Trip Cost | Min Profitable Divergence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lowest possible | 100 bps | 100 bps | 100 bps × 2 | ~4% | ~4.2% |
| Typical low epoch | 300 bps | 300 bps | 100 bps × 2 | ~8% | ~8.4% |
| Mixed epoch | 300 bps buy / 1400 bps sell | | 100 bps × 2 | ~19% | ~20% |
| High-tax epoch | 1400 bps | 1400 bps | 100 bps × 2 | ~30% | ~31.5% |

The lowest possible friction is approximately 4% (when both tokens are in their 100 bps minimum rate epoch). H025 used 8% as the minimum — this is correct for the typical case (300 bps ≈ 3% rates) but the absolute minimum is 4% (100 bps ≈ 1% rates).

**Critical observation:** The 100 bps minimum tax (1%) is the lowest possible rate per VRF derivation. For a profitable arbitrage in the absolute minimum case, the vault/AMM spread only needs to exceed ~4%. This is a tighter threshold than H025's stated ~8% minimum.

In practice, the cheap token's low rate (buy tax) is paired with its high sell rate. The arbitrage requires buying AND selling, and the directional pairing matters:

For Loop A (buy PROFIT, convert to CRIME, sell CRIME):
- Step 1: Buy PROFIT on AMM. PROFIT's tax rate depends on VRF — but PROFIT is NOT a faction token; it is only traded in the conversion vault and staking program. The AMM only has CRIME/SOL and FRAUD/SOL pools. PROFIT cannot be directly purchased on the protocol's AMM.

**This is a critical structural constraint not fully modeled in H025.**

### The PROFIT AMM Absence Constraint

The protocol's AMM pools are CRIME/SOL and FRAUD/SOL only. There is no PROFIT/SOL pool and no PROFIT/CRIME or PROFIT/FRAUD pool on the protocol's AMM. PROFIT is only:
- Received from conversion vault (CRIME/FRAUD → PROFIT)
- Spent at conversion vault (PROFIT → CRIME/FRAUD)
- Staked in the staking program

Loop A as described above (buy PROFIT cheaply on AMM) cannot be executed through the protocol's own AMM because PROFIT has no AMM pool. If PROFIT trades on external markets (e.g., Raydium, Jupiter), an external arbitrage loop becomes possible — but this depends on PROFIT having external market liquidity, which at launch it may not.

**For the protocol's internal architecture only**, the viable arbitrage loops are:

**Internal Loop B (CRIME/FRAUD undervalued vs. vault PROFIT rate):**
1. Buy CRIME cheaply on AMM (CRIME/SOL pool)
2. Convert 100 CRIME → 1 PROFIT at vault
3. [No way to sell PROFIT on AMM — requires external market or vault reverse]
4. Convert 1 PROFIT → 100 FRAUD at vault (if they want FRAUD)
5. Sell FRAUD on AMM (FRAUD/SOL pool)

This is the only internally completable loop: CRIME → (vault) → PROFIT → (vault) → FRAUD → (sell on AMM). Or FRAUD → PROFIT → CRIME in reverse. But the net effect is the attacker has converted CRIME to FRAUD via PROFIT as an intermediary, paying conversion dust on both steps. Unless CRIME and FRAUD have different AMM prices (which they normally do based on supply/demand and VRF tax asymmetry), this loop has no profit from the conversion itself — only from the CRIME/FRAUD price differential.

**The conversion vault as CRIME-FRAUD bridge:**
If CRIME is 10% undervalued relative to FRAUD on the AMM:
1. Buy CRIME cheaply (10% discount)
2. Convert CRIME → PROFIT → FRAUD (dust loss ≤ 2 base units per 100:1:100 loop)
3. Sell FRAUD at the higher price
4. Net: 10% gain - (buy tax on CRIME + sell tax on FRAUD + 2 AMM LP fees + vault dust)

This loop drains vault PROFIT then vault CRIME/FRAUD proportionally, but requires a persistent CRIME/FRAUD price differential AND the round-trip to be profitable after taxes.

### Vault Dust Accumulation

The conversion math uses integer division: `amount_in / CONVERSION_RATE`. For 100 CRIME → 1 PROFIT, any remainder (up to 99 CRIME base units) is lost to the vault as dust. This is documented in the architecture as "Conversion dust up to 99 base units." At 6 decimals, 99 base units = 0.000099 tokens — negligible per transaction but accumulates to the vault's benefit.

In the reverse direction (PROFIT → CRIME/FRAUD), `checked_mul(100)` has no dust.

### Vault Drain Scenarios Under Revised Analysis

Given the PROFIT AMM absence constraint, the drain scenarios are:

**Scenario 1 (External PROFIT Market):** If PROFIT lists on an external DEX, Loop A becomes viable: buy PROFIT externally at below-vault rate, convert to CRIME/FRAUD, sell on protocol AMM. Friction: ~4-8% internal + external DEX spread. At >4% PROFIT underpricing on external markets, vault PROFIT drains.

This is bounded by vault PROFIT supply (20M tokens minus dead stake) and the rate at which the arbitrage self-corrects as PROFIT price rises on the external market toward the vault's implied rate.

**Scenario 2 (CRIME/FRAUD Price Differential):** If CRIME and FRAUD diverge significantly, the CRIME→PROFIT→FRAUD bridge via vault becomes profitable. This requires a sustained price divergence exceeding the combined tax friction (~8-30% depending on epoch state).

**Scenario 3 (Low-Tax Epoch + Initial Launch Dislocation):** At genesis, pool seeding establishes an initial price. If the seeding creates a ≥4% divergence from the 100:1 vault rate, Loop B is immediately profitable at minimum-tax epochs. This is a launch-day risk window.

### Self-Correcting Nature (Confirmed from H025)

All three scenarios share the self-correcting property: each arbitrage loop trade moves the AMM price toward equilibrium with the vault rate. The constant-product curve (x × y = k) means price impact is proportional to trade size — larger loans create proportionally larger price movements, compressing the spread faster.

The binding constraint is pool depth at genesis. A shallow pool (low seeded reserves) creates large price impact per trade, correcting the spread quickly. A deeper pool allows more arbitrage cycles before equilibrium but also has more reserves to absorb the drain.

## Verdict

**CONFIRMED — LOW severity.**

This finding confirms H025's core conclusion — the drain risk exists but is self-correcting and bounded — while adding two refinements:

1. **The minimum friction threshold is ~4%, not ~8%.** At the 100 bps minimum tax rate (1%), round-trip arbitrage friction is approximately 4%, not 8%. H025 used the typical 300 bps (3%) case. Arbitrage becomes profitable at slightly lower price divergences than H025 modeled.

2. **The internal Loop A is not directly executable.** PROFIT has no AMM pool in the protocol. The canonical arbitrage loop (buy PROFIT on AMM → vault → sell CRIME) requires an external PROFIT market. Internal completable loops (CRIME → PROFIT → FRAUD bridge) have limited drain potential and require a persistent CRIME/FRAUD price differential exceeding ~8-30% depending on epoch state.

The severity remains LOW because:
- All loops are self-correcting (AMM constant-product closes the spread as trades execute)
- Vault is finite and deliberately seeded as one-time liquidity
- The most dangerous external-market scenario (Scenario 1) depends on external PROFIT liquidity existing — which is not present at genesis
- Pool depth at launch is the binding constraint, and the launch seeding plan creates reasonable depth

The 4% minimum friction (vs. H025's 8% claim) is a clarification, not a severity escalation. At 4% friction, an arbitrageur needs a 4.2% vault/market divergence for profitable grinding — this is still a meaningful threshold that the pool depth will correct quickly.

## Recommendations

### R1 — No Code Change Required (Same as H025 R4)

The conversion vault's finite, self-correcting arbitrage behavior is intentional. No rate limit or cooldown is required. Accept the design as-is for v1.4.

### R2 — Update Documentation to Reflect Correct Minimum Friction (Low Priority)

H025's stated "~8% minimum friction" understates the lowest possible case. Update internal documentation to note that at the 1% minimum tax epoch, round-trip friction can be as low as ~4%. This does not change the security posture but ensures accurate economic modeling.

### R3 — Monitor Vault Balances at Launch (Operational — Carried from H025 R3)

Set up alerts when vault PROFIT balance falls below 10%, 5%, and 1%. A rapid drain in the first hours after launch (before pool depth stabilizes) would indicate significant market dislocation at the launch seeding price. The most likely trigger is Scenario 3 (launch-day price dislocation), not sustained adversarial arbitrage.

### R4 — Verify Launch Pool Seeding Targets 100:1 Implied PROFIT Price (Pre-Launch Gate)

Before mainnet launch, verify that the pool seeding quantities imply a CRIME/SOL and FRAUD/SOL price that approximates the vault's implied 100:1 CRIME/PROFIT rate. If at launch the AMM implies 1 PROFIT = 80 CRIME (instead of 100), Loop B is immediately profitable and vault CRIME will drain. Verify: `AMM_CRIME_PRICE_IN_SOL × 100 ≈ AMM_PROFIT_PRICE_IN_SOL` (once an external PROFIT market exists). For the internal CRIME/FRAUD bridge, verify CRIME and FRAUD AMM prices are within the expected range at genesis.
