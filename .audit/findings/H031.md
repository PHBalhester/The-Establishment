# H031: Carnage Fund Accumulation — Large MEV Target

**Status:** CONFIRMED
**Severity:** MEDIUM
**Category:** MEV, Economic, Protocol Design
**Origin:** Novel

---

## Summary

The Carnage Fund SOL vault has no upper bound on its total balance. The 1000 SOL cap (`MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS`) is a **per-trigger spend cap**, not a vault ceiling. With a ~4.3% per-epoch trigger probability and continuous 24% tax inflow from all swaps, the vault can accumulate well beyond 1000 SOL between triggers. During the fallback execution window, the vault balance directly scales the dollar value exposed to the MEV sandwich described in H010. In extreme accumulation scenarios, the vault becomes an unusually high-value single PDA — an attractive target for any novel future exploit path.

---

## Analysis

### The Cap Is Per-Trigger, Not Total

The constant controlling maximum spend is:

```rust
// programs/epoch-program/src/constants.rs:151
pub const MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS: u64 = 1_000_000_000_000; // 1000 SOL in lamports
```

The execution logic at `carnage_execution.rs:261-265` reads:

```rust
let sol_balance = accounts.sol_vault.lamports();
let rent_exempt_min = rent.minimum_balance(0);
let available_sol = sol_balance.saturating_sub(rent_exempt_min);
let swap_amount = std::cmp::min(available_sol, MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS);
```

This is a `min()` against the available balance. If the vault holds 10,000 SOL, `swap_amount` is capped at 1,000 SOL — but 9,000 SOL remains in the vault, ready for the next trigger. There is no instruction that caps, drains, or redistributes the vault balance; it accumulates indefinitely.

### Continuous Inflow, Intermittent Outflow

Every swap through the Tax Program routes 24% of collected tax to the Carnage SOL vault (`tax-program/src/helpers/tax_math.rs:99-101`). This is unconditional — it flows every buy and sell regardless of epoch state. Outflow only occurs during Carnage execution (~4.3% trigger probability) and the 0.001 SOL epoch bounty (`TRIGGER_BOUNTY_LAMPORTS = 1_000_000`).

The bounty depletion is trivially small against 24% of all swap volume. The vault grows in expectation with every epoch that does not trigger, and even a triggering epoch only deploys up to 1,000 SOL of the balance.

### Accumulation Rate Modelling

The vault balance at any time is governed by:

```
balance_after_epoch = balance_before + carnage_inflow - (1000 SOL if triggered, else 0.001 SOL bounty)
```

Carnage inflow per epoch = `swap_volume_per_epoch × average_tax_rate × 0.24`

Representative estimates (mainnet, steady-state):

| Scenario | Avg tax | Daily volume | Carnage inflow/day | Expected triggers/day | Net accumulation/day |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low activity | 8.5% | 50 SOL | 1.02 SOL | 0.21 (≈ 4.3% × 48 epochs) | +0.81 SOL |
| Moderate activity | 8.5% | 500 SOL | 10.2 SOL | 0.21 | +8.0 SOL |
| High activity | 8.5% | 5,000 SOL | 102 SOL | 0.21 | +79.8 SOL |
| Viral/launch spike | 8.5% | 50,000 SOL | 1,020 SOL | 0.21 | +797 SOL |

At high activity, the vault grows by ~80 SOL/day on average. It would take ~12.5 days of high activity to accumulate 1,000 SOL from zero — after which every trigger only deploys 1,000 SOL, and continued trading immediately starts refilling the excess.

At viral-level volume (50,000 SOL/day traded), the vault can accumulate 1,000+ SOL within a single day. The 4.3% trigger probability means most of this excess is **never deployed** and simply sits in the vault.

### H010 MEV Amplification

H010 quantified the fallback-path sandwich extraction as `up to 25% of the Carnage swap amount`, bounded by `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS = 1,000 SOL`, giving a 250 SOL maximum. This analysis assumed the vault holds approximately 1,000 SOL. The vault accumulation finding does not change the per-trigger extraction ceiling (the cap still applies), so the 250 SOL maximum per fallback event remains correct.

However, the unlimited accumulation creates a different risk surface:

1. **Sustained MEV campaign**: Because the vault refills between triggers, a MEV actor can extract up to 250 SOL on every fallback Carnage execution, repeatedly, as long as the vault accumulates faster than triggers deplete it. The vault never drains to zero under any realistic volume scenario.

2. **Vault as novel exploit target**: A high-balance PDA (`carnage_sol_vault`, seeds = `[b"carnage_sol_vault"]`) is a known, deterministic address. At 5,000 SOL+ balance it becomes a compelling target for any future vulnerability — arithmetic bugs, CPI reentrancy, incorrect signer validation, or upgrade manipulation. The vault itself holds the value passively; it is not guarded by rate limits or withdrawal delays.

3. **Protocol-owned but economically opaque**: Locked SOL in the vault does not generate yield for stakers and does not appear to users as accessible. From a user's perspective, 24% of all their taxes flow into an accumulating fund that only partially deploys, with no cap on idle balance. At very high accumulation (e.g., 10,000+ SOL) this may be perceived as a protocol trust concern.

### No Vault Ceiling Anywhere in the Codebase

A search across all epoch-program instructions confirms no mechanism caps the vault balance:
- `initialize_carnage_fund`: Creates the vault PDA with 0 balance.
- `trigger_epoch_transition`: Reads vault balance only for the bounty check; does not redistribute excess.
- `consume_randomness`: Does not reference the vault.
- `execute_carnage` / `execute_carnage_atomic`: Deploys up to 1,000 SOL; does not redistribute excess.
- `expire_carnage`: Clears state; does not move funds.

There is no `rebalance_carnage_vault`, `cap_carnage_vault`, or equivalent instruction.

## Quantitative Assessment

### Vault Balance Scenarios at Different Activity Levels

**Assumption**: Vault starts at 0; no triggers fire for 30 days (adversarial suppression combined with low trigger probability).

| Daily Volume | Carnage Inflow/Day | Balance After 30 Days |
|---|---|---|
| 50 SOL | 1.02 SOL | ~30.6 SOL |
| 500 SOL | 10.2 SOL | ~306 SOL |
| 5,000 SOL | 102 SOL | ~3,060 SOL |
| 50,000 SOL | 1,020 SOL | ~30,600 SOL |

At viral launch volume and sustained Carnage suppression (H009 being exploited), the vault could accumulate tens of thousands of SOL with no natural pressure to deploy it. Even without suppression, the 4.3% trigger rate means statistically 28/30 days have no trigger — allowing steady buildup.

### Maximum Theoretical Vault Balance

There is no on-chain ceiling. Maximum is bounded only by:
- Total protocol swap volume (inflow rate)
- Market duration
- Trigger frequency

At 50,000 SOL/day volume over a 90-day protocol lifecycle with 4.3% trigger rate: expected accumulated excess above the deployed 1,000 SOL is approximately `1,020 SOL/day × 90 - (0.21 triggers/day × 1,000 SOL × 90) ≈ 91,800 - 18,900 = 72,900 SOL`. This is a theoretical ceiling under extreme volume, not a typical expectation.

### Incremental MEV Risk Above H010

The per-trigger MEV ceiling (250 SOL) is unchanged by this finding. The incremental contribution is:
- **Frequency multiplier**: The vault never depletes below the 1,000 SOL trigger threshold at moderate/high volume, so every Carnage fallback event is a full 250 SOL extraction opportunity.
- **Longevity of target**: A vault that permanently holds 1,000–10,000 SOL creates a persistent high-value target surface for the lifetime of the protocol.

## Verdict

**CONFIRMED — MEDIUM severity.**

The vault has no total balance cap; the 1,000 SOL constant is a per-trigger spend limit only. At any non-trivial trading volume, the vault will hold a multiple of 1,000 SOL in steady state. This is not in itself a direct exploitable vulnerability — the vault is a PDA with correct seed derivation and no unauthorized withdrawal path currently known. The finding is rated MEDIUM (not HIGH) because:

1. There is no immediate attack: the vault's balance does not by itself create a new exploit vector. Existing attack paths (H009 suppression, H010 MEV sandwich) already account for the vault being substantial.
2. The per-trigger extraction ceiling from H010 remains 250 SOL regardless of vault size — the cap on deployment bounds each individual MEV event.
3. The vault balance's effect on future exploit paths is speculative, not demonstrated.

The severity warrants medium attention rather than critical because the root concern is the persistent high-value target posture rather than a direct fund-loss mechanism today.

## Recommendations

### 1. Introduce a Vault High-Water Cap (Protocol Parameter)

Add a constant `MAX_CARNAGE_VAULT_LAMPORTS` (e.g., 3,000–5,000 SOL) and check in `swap_sol_buy`/`swap_sol_sell` or in the tax distribution: if the vault already exceeds the cap, redirect the Carnage portion to staking or treasury instead of the vault.

```rust
// In tax_math distribution:
if sol_vault.lamports() < MAX_CARNAGE_VAULT_LAMPORTS {
    transfer carnage_amount -> sol_vault;
} else {
    // overflow: add to staking escrow
    transfer carnage_amount -> staking_escrow;
}
```

This caps accumulation risk without changing the trigger mechanics. The 1,000 SOL per-trigger spend cap can remain.

### 2. Trigger-On-Threshold as Alternative

Rather than (or in addition to) a vault cap, consider a secondary trigger condition: if `sol_vault.lamports() >= MAX_CARNAGE_VAULT_LAMPORTS`, the next `consume_randomness` forces a Carnage trigger regardless of VRF byte 5. This deploys excess capital rather than idling it and creates natural self-regulating behavior.

### 3. Documentation Clarity

The `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS` constant comment (`constants.rs:148-150`) reads: "Bounds compute requirements and prevents 'too big to execute' failures." This does not communicate that it is a per-trigger cap, not a vault ceiling. Add explicit documentation that the vault itself is uncapped, and note the design intent if this is intentional.

### 4. Monitor Vault Balance in Protocol Dashboard

Expose vault SOL balance prominently in the frontend. Transparency allows the community to assess accumulation and reduces the perception risk of a large, opaque locked fund.

### 5. Consider Jito Bundle Execution for Fallback

(Reinforcing H010 Recommendation 5) If the vault routinely holds >1,000 SOL, the fallback MEV window becomes an even higher-stakes event. Executing fallback Carnage via Jito bundles from the protocol's crank service directly addresses both H010 and the incremental exposure from this finding.
