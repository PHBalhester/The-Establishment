# S002: Carnage Fund Accumulation Under Suppression — Deferred Shock Risk

**Status:** CONFIRMED
**Severity:** HIGH
**Category:** Economic, State Machine
**Inspired by:** H009 (CONFIRMED), H014 (CONFIRMED)

## Summary

Under sustained Carnage suppression (via H009), the Carnage Fund vault accumulates 24% of all protocol swap taxes indefinitely without ever discharging. When suppression is eventually lifted — either by a protocol upgrade fixing H009, by the attacker stopping their bot, or by the protocol team operating the crank directly — the vault potentially holds a very large SOL balance. The next triggered Carnage event will attempt to deploy `min(vault_balance, MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS)` in a single swap. This investigation examines: (1) how fast the vault accumulates, (2) what the single-event price impact looks like when it finally fires, and (3) whether the deferred discharge creates an exploitable predictable event.

## Analysis

### Accumulation Rate Model

From `tax_math.rs:79-110`:

```rust
const CARNAGE_BPS: u128 = 2_400;  // 24%
// carnage = floor(total * 2400 / 10000)
let carnage_u128 = total.checked_mul(CARNAGE_BPS)?.checked_div(BPS_DENOM)?;
```

The vault receives a fixed 24% floor division of every lamport of swap tax collected. This accumulates with:
- Every `swap_sol_buy` and `swap_sol_sell` call through the Tax Program
- The 71/24/5 split is enforced unconditionally — there is no "pause accumulation" mechanism
- Even when Carnage is suppressed, the 24% allocation continues flowing to the vault

**Accumulation formula:**
```
vault_growth_per_epoch = sum_of_all_swap_taxes_in_epoch × 0.24
```

Under suppression, `vault_growth` compounds each epoch with zero discharge.

### Accumulated Balance Scenarios

Assuming modest mainnet volume estimates:

| Daily SOL swap volume | Avg tax rate | Daily tax collected | Daily Carnage accumulation | Days to 100 SOL vault |
|-----------------------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------------------|----------------------|
| 500 SOL/day | 8.5% (midpoint) | 42.5 SOL | 10.2 SOL | ~10 days |
| 2000 SOL/day | 8.5% | 170 SOL | 40.8 SOL | ~2.5 days |
| 5000 SOL/day | 8.5% | 425 SOL | 102 SOL | ~1 day |

For a protocol achieving even modest trading volume, the vault can accumulate 100-500 SOL within a week of sustained suppression — and the 1,000,000 SOL `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS` cap is effectively unreachable in practice (it would require ~100,000 SOL/day for a month of suppression).

### CarnageFundState: No Emergency Drain Path

From `carnage_fund_state.rs:68-132`, the `CarnageFundState` tracks:

```rust
pub struct CarnageFundState {
    pub sol_vault: Pubkey,
    pub crime_vault: Pubkey,
    pub fraud_vault: Pubkey,
    pub held_token: u8,
    pub held_amount: u64,
    pub last_trigger_epoch: u32,
    pub total_sol_spent: u64,
    pub total_crime_burned: u64,
    pub total_fraud_burned: u64,
    pub total_triggers: u32,
    pub initialized: bool,
    pub bump: u8,
}
```

Crucially, there is **no admin drain instruction**, no maximum vault cap enforcement, and no governance mechanism to redirect accumulated SOL. The only mechanism to discharge the vault is a Carnage execution — which itself is capped at `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS` (1,000,000 SOL in lamports = 1,000 SOL). If the vault holds more than 1,000 SOL, only 1,000 SOL discharges per trigger.

This creates an interesting secondary state: after months of suppression, the vault could hold thousands of SOL that trickles out 1,000 SOL per trigger (at ~4.3% probability per epoch), taking many epochs to fully deploy even after H009 is fixed.

### Deferred Shock: Price Impact Analysis

When suppression lifts and the next Carnage trigger fires, the constant-product price impact of a large single-event buy is significant.

Assume at the moment of Carnage trigger:
- Token pool reserves: 1,000 SOL / 500M tokens (hypothetical equilibrium)
- Carnage buy amount: 1,000 SOL (vault balance, capped by `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS`)

Using constant-product formula:
```
tokens_out = reserve_token × buy_amount / (reserve_sol + buy_amount)
           = 500M × 1000 / (1000 + 1000)
           = 250M tokens (50% of pool drained in one swap)
```

Price impact: buying 1,000 SOL worth drives price from 2,000 SOL/B tokens → 4,000 SOL/B tokens = **100% price spike in a single transaction.**

With a smaller pool (e.g., 500 SOL / 500M tokens at thin early-mainnet liquidity):
```
tokens_out = 500M × 1000 / (500 + 1000) = 333M tokens (66% of pool drained)
```

This is an extreme price dislocation event.

### Deferred Shock as MEV Event

The deferred shock is **predictable and exploitable** once suppression lifts:

1. The vault's SOL balance is readable on-chain (SystemAccount lamports at the `sol_vault` PDA address).
2. `EpochState.carnage_pending` transitions from false → true atomically in `consume_randomness`.
3. **The moment `carnage_pending` becomes true**, any on-chain observer knows:
   - Carnage will deploy `min(vault_balance, MAX_CAP)` SOL
   - The target token is readable from `EpochState.carnage_target`
   - The lock window is 50 slots

This is the H010 MEV scenario but with a vastly larger buy amount. A 1,000 SOL Carnage buy gives the H010 attacker up to 250 SOL of extraction opportunity (25% fallback tolerance). With a vault that has been accumulating for months, this single event could be one of the largest protocol-internal swaps the pool has ever seen.

**Deferred shock MEV formula:**
```
max_extraction_per_deferred_trigger =
    min(vault_balance, MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS) × 0.25  (fallback 75% floor)
```

For a 1,000 SOL vault: up to 250 SOL extractable in the first fallback-path execution of the pent-up Carnage. This is the maximum scenario from H010, not the routine case.

### The "Fix Day" Vulnerability Window

When the team deploys the fix for H009 (making `carnage_state` mandatory), this creates a known future event:
- The fix deploy is observable on-chain (program upgrade)
- From that upgrade block forward, Carnage is guaranteed to be evaluated
- Vault balance is publicly known at all times
- The next epoch boundary after the fix fires the first unmanipulated `consume_randomness`
- If byte[5] < 11 on that epoch, the deferred vault deploys

An attacker watching for the H009 fix deploy can:
1. Observe the upgrade
2. Read vault balance
3. Begin monitoring for the next epoch transition
4. Be pre-positioned in the pool BEFORE the Carnage trigger can fire
5. Execute a sandwich on the first triggered Carnage after the fix

This is not hypothetical — this is a concrete sequence that follows mechanically from the fix deployment, and the profit opportunity is proportional to how long the vault was suppressed.

### Vault Does Not Auto-Rebalance

There is no mechanism in any program to:
- Cap the vault balance
- Redirect excess accumulation to staking
- Trigger partial Carnage execution at lower amounts
- Split a large vault balance across multiple transactions

The only discharge path is a full single-event Carnage trigger using `min(vault_balance, MAX_CAP)`. This all-or-nothing design makes the deferred shock scenario worse.

## Code Evidence

**24% accumulation never pauses** (`tax_math.rs:99-101`):
```rust
let carnage_u128 = total.checked_mul(CARNAGE_BPS)?.checked_div(BPS_DENOM)?;
let carnage = u64::try_from(carnage_u128).ok()?;
```

**MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS caps each event at 1B lamports = 1,000 SOL** (from `constants.rs` as referenced in H010):
```
MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS = 1_000_000_000_000  // Wait — H010 cites 1,000 SOL cap
```

Note from H010 code evidence: `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS = 1_000_000_000_000` (1 trillion lamports = 1,000,000 SOL). H010's analysis used a 1,000 SOL figure as a realistic scenario. The actual cap is 1,000,000 SOL — effectively unbounded for any realistic vault balance. This means there is NO cap preventing the full vault from deploying in a single Carnage event, regardless of accumulated balance. This is more severe than described above.

Correction to analysis: with a vault holding 1,000 SOL and `MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS = 1,000,000 SOL`, the **entire vault deploys in one transaction**. The deferred shock is uncapped.

**Carnage buy amount calculation** (`carnage_execution.rs:260-265`):
```rust
let sol_balance = accounts.sol_vault.lamports();
let rent_exempt_min = rent.minimum_balance(0);
let available_sol = sol_balance.saturating_sub(rent_exempt_min);
let swap_amount = std::cmp::min(available_sol, MAX_CARNAGE_SWAP_LAMPORTS);
```

This deploys `available_sol` (entire vault minus rent minimum) in one buy, bounded only by the very high cap.

**CarnageFundState has no governance drain** (`carnage_fund_state.rs`): No admin authority field, no drain instruction, no max balance cap, no emergency pause.

## Verdict

**CONFIRMED — HIGH severity.**

The deferred accumulation problem is a direct, confirmed consequence of H009. The combination creates a secondary risk that persists even after H009 is fixed:

1. **During suppression**: vault traps 24% of all protocol taxes. SOL accumulates indefinitely without productive deployment. Stakers indirectly harmed (the 24% that would have reinforced token price support sits idle).

2. **At lift/fix**: a predictable, extremely large Carnage event is telegraphed on-chain. The vault balance is publicly readable. This creates a supercharged H010 MEV opportunity at precisely the most visible moment in the protocol's fix deployment.

3. **Post-lift mechanics**: the constant-product pool cannot absorb a sudden 100-1000 SOL Carnage buy without extreme price impact. Depending on pool depth, this could cause secondary cascading effects (arbitrageurs pulling liquidity, stop-losses triggering, bonding curve price dislocation).

Severity is HIGH (not CRITICAL) because the vault SOL is not lost — it will eventually deploy correctly. But the size of the deferred shock and the exploitability of the "fix day" window are material risks that the fix for H009 must account for.

## Recommendations

1. **Primary**: Fix H009 immediately (mandatory `carnage_state`). This stops further accumulation under the attack scenario.

2. **Add a vault balance cap**: Consider implementing a maximum vault balance (e.g., 100 SOL) above which excess tax revenue is redirected to the staking distribution instead of the vault. This bounds the deferred shock regardless of suppression duration.

3. **Partial Carnage deployment**: Modify `carnage_execution.rs` to deploy at most a configurable `CARNAGE_MAX_PER_EPOCH` amount (e.g., 50 SOL) regardless of vault balance, spreading large buys across multiple epochs. This preserves market impact smoothness and reduces MEV extraction opportunity per event.

4. **Post-fix graduated deployment**: If a large vault balance has accumulated by the time H009 is fixed, consider a governance instruction (via Squads timelock) to stage the vault deployment over N epochs rather than allowing the first triggered Carnage to dump the full balance into the pool.

5. **Monitor vault balance publicly**: The UI should display the current Carnage Fund vault balance. This allows the community to observe accumulation anomalies and serves as a canary for active suppression attacks.

6. **Fix day coordination**: When deploying the H009 fix, ensure the protocol team's crank service is running and the first `consume_randomness` post-fix uses the atomic path. This minimizes the H010 fallback MEV window during the first deferred discharge.
