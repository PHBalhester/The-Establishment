## Summary

The conversion vault exchanges CRIME/FRAUD for PROFIT (and vice versa) at a hardcoded 100:1 rate with no oracle, no dynamic pricing, and no rate-limit. When the AMM market price diverges from this fixed rate, a bidirectional arbitrage loop becomes profitable. The concern is that an arbitrageur could drain the vault's entire PROFIT supply (or CRIME/FRAUD supply) before equilibrium is restored.

After full code and economic analysis, the risk is real but naturally bounded and self-correcting. The vault is not a pool with continuous liquidity — it is a finite reserve deliberately seeded at protocol launch and not replenished. The drain risk is proportional to vault holdings, not to market size, and the swap tax friction is sufficient to prevent non-profitable grinding.

## Analysis

### 1. Vault Architecture

The vault has three PDA-held token accounts:
- `vault_crime`: seeded with 250,000,000 CRIME (250M human units)
- `vault_fraud`: seeded with 250,000,000 FRAUD (250M human units)
- `vault_profit`: seeded with `20,000,000 PROFIT - 1` (effectively all 20M PROFIT minus dead stake)

Source: `scripts/deploy/initialize.ts:118-121`

```typescript
const VAULT_SEED_CRIME  = 250_000_000_000_000; // 250M CRIME
const VAULT_SEED_FRAUD  = 250_000_000_000_000; // 250M FRAUD
const VAULT_SEED_PROFIT = 20_000_000_000_000 - MINIMUM_STAKE; // 20M PROFIT minus 1 dead stake
```

The vault is seeded once at initialization. There is no mechanism to replenish it on-chain. Once drained, that path of the vault is inoperative — conversions in that direction fail with an SPL Token insufficient-funds error.

### 2. Conversion Math

```rust
// convert.rs:101-113
if (*input_mint == *crime || *input_mint == *fraud) && *output_mint == *profit {
    // CRIME/FRAUD -> PROFIT: divide by 100
    let out = amount_in / CONVERSION_RATE;
    require!(out > 0, VaultError::OutputTooSmall);
    Ok(out)
} else if *input_mint == *profit && (*output_mint == *crime || *output_mint == *fraud) {
    // PROFIT -> CRIME/FRAUD: multiply by 100
    amount_in
        .checked_mul(CONVERSION_RATE)
        .ok_or_else(|| error!(VaultError::MathOverflow))
}
```

`CONVERSION_RATE = 100` is hardcoded. No slippage parameter, no cooldown, no per-user limit, no daily cap.

### 3. Arbitrage Conditions

Two loops exist depending on market conditions:

**Loop A — CRIME/FRAUD overvalued vs PROFIT (market rate > 100:1):**
1. Buy PROFIT cheaply on AMM (PROFIT cheap relative to CRIME)
2. Convert PROFIT → 100 CRIME via vault
3. Sell CRIME on AMM for more SOL than spent
4. Repeat until vault PROFIT depleted or price reaches equilibrium

**Loop B — CRIME/FRAUD undervalued vs PROFIT (market rate < 100:1):**
1. Buy CRIME cheaply on AMM
2. Convert 100 CRIME → 1 PROFIT via vault
3. Sell PROFIT on AMM for more SOL than spent
4. Repeat until vault CRIME/FRAUD depleted or price reaches equilibrium

### 4. Friction Analysis — Is Arbitrage Profitable After Costs?

Each loop leg incurs:
- **AMM LP fee**: 1% per swap (`SOL_POOL_FEE_BPS = 100` in `amm/src/constants.rs:15`)
- **Tax Program tax**: 3-14% depending on epoch state

A round trip (AMM buy + vault convert + AMM sell) incurs at minimum **~8% friction** (two AMM swaps × (1% LP + 3% buy/sell tax minimum)) and at most **~30% friction** at 14% sell tax epoch. For arbitrage to be profitable, the market/vault price divergence must exceed this friction band.

At genesis the pools are seeded such that the implied market rate approximates the vault rate. As trading occurs and prices diverge, the vault acts as a pressure valve — the friction band means only divergences >8% generate extractable profit. Below that threshold, the arbitrage is loss-making.

**Minimum profitable divergence: ~8-10%** (low-tax epoch)
**Friction at high-tax epoch: ~28-30%** (14% sell + 14% buy + 2% AMM fees)

### 5. Maximum Drain Scenario

**Worst case: Loop A, low-tax epoch (8% friction), prices diverge by exactly 10%:**
- Attacker can extract approximately 2% of each conversion cycle as profit
- Vault PROFIT supply: 20M PROFIT
- To drain all 20M PROFIT: attacker needs 20M PROFIT worth of CRIME/FRAUD to convert
- CRIME supply: 1B total, 250M in vault already; AMM pool is seeded with a fraction
- Each loop cycle moves market price toward equilibrium (selling CRIME on AMM lowers CRIME price)
- Vault naturally self-corrects: as CRIME price falls toward 100:1 PROFIT ratio, the loop becomes unprofitable before vault is fully drained

In practice, a full vault drain would require:
1. A sustained extreme divergence (>30%) to remain profitable throughout
2. Sufficient AMM pool depth to absorb hundreds of millions of tokens
3. The loop to be sustainable before the market self-corrects

The AMM pool depth is the binding constraint. The CRIME/SOL pool is seeded with a fraction of total supply. Large sales from the arbitrage loop would push CRIME price down rapidly (constant-product curve), compressing the spread before the vault is meaningfully drained.

### 6. No Rate-Limit or Cooldown

There is no per-user rate limit, no global daily conversion cap, and no cooldown period on the `convert` instruction. A single bot could issue back-to-back transactions in a single epoch. However, this only amplifies the natural drain if the spread exists — it does not create a spread where none exists.

### 7. Comparison to Prior Art

Fixed-rate conversion vaults are common in DeFi (e.g., MakerDAO's PSM, Curve's stable pools at pegs). In well-designed systems, the finite vault supply and arbitrage friction together bound the maximum extractable value to the vault's seeded liquidity × (divergence − friction). The vault here is explicitly a one-way liquidity provision, not a two-sided market maker.

## Verdict

**Confirmed — Intended Design with Bounded, Acceptable Risk.**

The fixed 100:1 rate is a deliberate protocol design choice serving as a permissionless price-stability mechanism: it provides a hard anchor for the CRIME/FRAUD-to-PROFIT exchange ratio and allows users to convert freely without an oracle. The arbitrage drain risk is:

1. **Self-limiting**: Each arbitrage trade moves the market price toward equilibrium, reducing the spread and ultimately making further arbitrage unprofitable.
2. **Friction-bounded**: At the minimum tax rate epoch (3% buy + 3% sell + 1% + 1% AMM = ~8%), only divergences exceeding ~8% are profitable. At normal or high-tax epochs the threshold is 15-30%.
3. **Supply-bounded**: The vault is seeded once. The maximum extractable value is bounded by vault holdings, which are protocol-controlled initial allocations, not user deposits.
4. **Pool-depth-bounded**: AMM constant-product mechanics cause rapid price impact on large trades, compressing the spread faster than the vault can be drained.

The original Tier 2 classification overstates the risk. The token/economic audit context (`05-token-economic.md:60`) already notes "Conversion vault lacks rate limit or cooldown" as a medium finding, and the economic model context (`09-economic-model.md`) classifies it as "Low difficulty" with impact "proportional to market conditions."

The primary residual risk is **rapid full drain during an extreme market dislocation** (e.g., a coordinated pump to >30% divergence) in the first hours after launch before pool depth is established. This window is brief and the protocol design accepts this as a feature (the vault is intended to be drained by the market finding equilibrium).

## Recommendations

### R1 — Document Vault Drain as Expected Behavior (Low Priority)
Add explicit documentation that the conversion vault is a finite one-way liquidity provision that will be progressively drained by market arbitrage. Users relying on vault conversions should be aware the vault may be empty at any time.

### R2 — Consider Optional Per-Epoch Conversion Cap (Design Decision)
A soft global cap (e.g., 1M PROFIT per epoch) could smooth the drain over multiple epochs rather than allowing a single actor to drain it in one session. This is a product tradeoff: lower caps reduce arbitrage efficiency as a price anchor; higher caps approach current unlimited behavior. **This is a design decision for the protocol owner, not a security requirement.**

### R3 — Monitor Vault Balance at Launch (Operational)
Set up monitoring alerts when vault PROFIT balance falls below 10%, 5%, and 1% thresholds. A rapid drain in the first epochs post-launch would indicate significant market dislocation and may warrant a governance response.

### R4 — Accept Risk As-Is (Recommended for v1.4)
Given the friction-bounded, self-correcting nature of the drain, and the intentional design of the vault as a finite liquidity provision, no code change is required. Document the behavior and proceed.
