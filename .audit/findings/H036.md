## Summary

The Conversion Vault provides a fixed-rate 100:1 exchange between CRIME/FRAUD and PROFIT with no per-user or per-epoch rate limit. A user could convert large amounts of CRIME or FRAUD into PROFIT in a single transaction, consuming a significant portion of the vault's PROFIT balance. However, this behaviour is by design, and several structural constraints reduce the risk to a level appropriate for the protocol's architecture.

---

## Analysis

### What the code does

`convert.rs:116-174` executes unconditional bi-directional swaps:

1. `compute_output_with_mints` (lines 101-113) calculates output purely from `amount_in / CONVERSION_RATE` (CRIME/FRAUD→PROFIT) or `amount_in * CONVERSION_RATE` (PROFIT→CRIME/FRAUD). No per-user limit, no per-epoch limit, no cooldown.
2. The `VaultConfig` struct (`state.rs`) stores only `bump: u8`. There is no `last_conversion_slot`, no `epoch_conversion_volume`, and no per-user state account.
3. The `Convert` accounts struct (`convert.rs:9-54`) requires only `user` (signer), `vault_config`, the two token accounts for the user, and the two vault token accounts. No guard account is required that could carry rate-limit state.

### Vault capacity

At deployment (`initialize.ts:119-121`):
- Vault PROFIT balance: `20_000_000_000_000 − 1_000_000 = 19,999,999,000,000` base units (≈ **19,999,999 PROFIT**)
- Vault CRIME balance: `250,000,000,000,000` base units (250M CRIME)
- Vault FRAUD balance: `250,000,000,000,000` base units (250M FRAUD)

Total PROFIT supply is 20M tokens (the entire supply). All PROFIT in existence is routed through:
- Staking dead stake: 1 PROFIT (permanently locked)
- Conversion vault: ~20M PROFIT

This means the vault holds **≈100% of the circulating PROFIT supply** at launch.

### Rate-limit absence — what an attacker could do

A user holding sufficient CRIME or FRAUD (which have 1B token supplies) could:
1. Acquire X CRIME tokens (e.g., 200M CRIME from the AMM or bonding curve)
2. Call `convert` in one transaction with `amount_in = 200,000,000,000,000` (200M base units)
3. Receive `200,000,000,000,000 / 100 = 2,000,000,000,000` PROFIT (2M PROFIT)
4. Repeat until vault PROFIT is exhausted

There is no on-chain mechanism preventing this. An attacker with sufficient CRIME/FRAUD could drain the vault's PROFIT supply across multiple transactions.

### Why severity is Low, not High

Several structural factors substantially mitigate the threat:

**1. PROFIT is not independently valuable — it gates staking yield**
PROFIT's primary utility is staking for SOL yield. Draining the vault does not steal protocol-owned SOL; it merely concentrates PROFIT ownership. The staker then competes against their own stake for the same reward pool. The SOL rewards in the staking escrow are unaffected by vault drainage.

**2. The conversion is bidirectional — PROFIT can flow back**
The vault accepts PROFIT→CRIME/FRAUD at the same 100:1 rate. Any PROFIT drained out can be returned. There is no mechanism that makes drainage one-directional or permanent. A drained vault can be refilled by anyone holding PROFIT.

**3. Cost of attack scales with PROFIT acquired — not cheap**
Draining the full 20M PROFIT requires converting 2 billion CRIME or FRAUD tokens (2,000,000,000 tokens at 6 decimals = `2,000,000,000,000,000` base units). With a total CRIME supply of 1B tokens and 250M already in the vault, acquiring that volume requires buying a substantial fraction of the entire supply through the AMM, which would move the price enormously and make the attack economically self-defeating.

**4. The vault is explicitly first-come-first-served by design**
The architecture memo (`ARCHITECTURE.md:319-320`) classifies `convert (Vault)` as user-accessible with risk level LOW, noting it is "bounded by vault balance." The token-economic context (`05-token-economic.md:4`) identifies this limitation but rates it at the same tier as other low observations.

**5. Transfer Hook whitelist enforces legitimate actors**
CRIME and FRAUD tokens require both source and destination to be on the whitelist for Token-2022 transfers. The vault's CRIME/FRAUD token accounts are whitelisted (`initialize.ts:1976`). Arbitrary fresh wallets cannot trivially acquire and convert large volumes without already being integrated into the protocol's whitelist ecosystem.

**6. No direct user harm beyond opportunity loss**
If the vault is drained, other users simply cannot convert CRIME/FRAUD to PROFIT until either (a) the vault is refilled, or (b) they use the AMM to acquire PROFIT directly. It is inconvenience, not loss of principal.

### What is missing (not a vulnerability, but a design gap)

There is no per-epoch conversion cap and no admin refill mechanism other than a manual token transfer. If the vault becomes permanently depleted (e.g., a whale legitimately accumulates most PROFIT for staking), there is no on-chain path to refund it. Refilling requires the admin to hold free PROFIT and manually transfer it to `vault_profit`. This is an operational concern, not a security vulnerability.

## Verdict

**Confirmed with downgraded severity: Low.**

The absence of a rate limit is a deliberate economic design choice for a first-come-first-served mechanism. The original Tier 2 designation overstates the risk because:

- Draining PROFIT does not extract SOL or any staked value.
- The conversion is fully reversible at the same rate.
- The cost to drain the vault is commensurate with acquiring a large fraction of the entire CRIME/FRAUD supply, which would be economically irrational due to AMM price impact.
- Transfer Hook whitelist enforcement limits who can participate.

The finding is real (no rate limit exists, vault could be depleted), but the impact is opportunity denial rather than direct financial loss, and the economic cost of attack exceeds any realistic benefit.

---

## Recommendations

**R1 (Low priority — consider for v1.5):** Add a per-epoch global conversion cap to the `VaultConfig`, e.g., `max_profit_out_per_epoch: u64` and `epoch_profit_out: u64` with a reset slot. This would prevent a single actor from draining the vault within one epoch, preserving fair access for all participants without changing the economic model.

**R2 (Low priority):** Document in the admin runbook that the vault PROFIT balance should be monitored and refilled if it drops below a threshold (e.g., 1M PROFIT). Since PROFIT→CRIME/FRAUD is always available, an admin holding PROFIT can refill via a simple token transfer.

**R3 (Not recommended):** Do NOT add per-user conversion limits. The whitelist mechanism already restricts participants to protocol-integrated actors, and per-user limits would add state rent overhead without meaningful protection against a determined actor using multiple wallets.
