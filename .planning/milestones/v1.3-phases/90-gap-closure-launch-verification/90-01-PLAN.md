  <action>
    Create `Docs/tax-mev-defense.md` documenting why the asymmetric tax structure serves as MEV defense. This closes H015 by documenting the rationale rather than changing the design.

    Content should cover:
    1. **Problem**: On DEXes, sandwich bots extract value by front-running and back-running user trades. This is especially severe on Solana where block producers can reorder transactions.
    2. **How taxes defend against MEV**: The variable epoch-based tax (ranging from ~3% to ~14%) on every SOL pool swap makes sandwich attacks unprofitable. A bot must pay tax on both the front-run buy AND the back-run sell. Even at the minimum 3% rate, the round-trip tax cost (6%+ of position size) exceeds any slippage profit the bot could extract from a user's trade.
    3. **Asymmetric rates amplify defense**: When buy tax is high and sell tax is low (or vice versa), the bot faces worst-case tax on at least one leg of the sandwich. The randomized epoch-based tax flipping means bots cannot predict which leg will be expensive.
    4. **Why this is better than other MEV defenses**: No reliance on Jito bundles, no MEV-aware routing, no off-chain relayers. The tax is on-chain and unavoidable — it applies to every trade including bot trades. The defense is a natural property of the protocol design, not an added feature.
    5. **Trade-off acknowledged**: Legitimate users also pay the tax. This is the intended design — the tax funds the yield system (PROFIT staking rewards, Carnage burns). The MEV defense is a beneficial side effect, not the primary purpose.

    Reference: `Docs/tax-spec.md` for tax rate details, `Docs/security-model.md` for broader security context.
