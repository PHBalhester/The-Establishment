**Section 12 (Security Considerations, ~line 1342) -- GAP-14:**
- REMOVE Section 12.1 (whitelist bypass analysis) -- no whitelist
- REMOVE Section 12.3 (Privy friction analysis) -- no Privy
- ADD new security analysis subsections:
  - **Sell Manipulation / Wash Trading:** A sell+rebuy cycle costs >= 15% of position value. Example: buy 10M tokens at average price X, sell for 85% of X, rebuy costs X again. Net loss: 15%. Makes sandwich attacks a losing proposition on the curve. The attacker would need to front-run a large buy, buying tokens before the victim, then sell after -- but the 15% tax on sell wipes any profit unless the victim's purchase moves the price by >17.6% (nearly impossible with 460M token supply).
  - **Cap Enforcement Without Whitelist:** ATA balance reads are safe because Transfer Hook prevents wallet-to-wallet token transfers during the curve phase. Users cannot shuffle tokens to circumvent the 20M cap. However, a user CAN create multiple wallets and buy up to 20M on each. This is the intended design -- the 20M cap limits single-wallet concentration, not multi-wallet sybil attacks. Full sybil resistance would require KYC (rejected as counter to crypto ethos).
  - **Burn-and-Claim Solvency Proof:** The refund formula `(user_tokens / total_outstanding) * vault_balance` is provably solvent. After each claim, both numerator (user's tokens burned) and denominator (total outstanding reduced) and vault balance (reduced by refund amount) maintain the invariant that all remaining holders can claim their full proportional share. Mathematical proof: sum of all possible refunds = vault balance (by construction). See worked example in Section 8.
  - **Tax Escrow Integrity:** The tax escrow PDA is program-controlled. Only the bonding curve program can transfer SOL out of it, via `consolidate_for_refund` (failure path) or `distribute_tax_escrow` (success path). External actors cannot drain it.
  - **Deadline Manipulation:** The 48-hour deadline uses slot numbers (Solana clock), not wall-clock time. Slot times average ~400ms but can vary. The deadline should be set conservatively (48 hours * 2.5 slots/sec = 432,000 slots). Validators cannot meaningfully manipulate the slot clock.
- Keep Section 12.2 (front-running analysis) if it exists but update for sell mechanics
